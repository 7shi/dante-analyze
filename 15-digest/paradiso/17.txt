# Canto 17 — The Prophecy of Exile and the Vision of the Great Lombard

## Scene 1-6: The Mythological Parallel
en: In *ella*, *Dante* compares himself to the mentioned *Faetone*, who visited *Climenè* to confirm his lineage as *li padri* often leave *ai figli* wanting, and recalls how *Beatrice* had perceived him.
ja: そのなかでDanteは、li padriがしばしばai figliに欠落を残すために自らの血統を確かめようとClimenèを訪ねた前述のFaetoneに己を重ね、Beatriceが自分をどのように見ていたかを回想する。

## Scene 7-12: Beatrice's Instruction
en: In *ella*, *Beatrice* urges *Dante* to voice his inner longing, not to inform her, but so that the mentioned *Cacciaguida* may be able to answer him.
ja: BeatriceはDanteに、内なる切望を口にするよう促す。それは彼女に伝えるためではなく、前述のCacciaguidaが彼に答えられるようにするためであった。

## Scene 13-27: Dante's Plea for Knowledge
en: In *ella*, *Dante* speaks of the divine foresight of *Beatrice* compared to *le terrene menti* and recalls grave warnings from *Virgilio* regarding the *anime* and his future, seeking knowledge of the fate that awaits him.
ja: Danteは彼女の中で、le terrene mentiとは対照的なBeatriceの神聖な予見について語り、animeと自らの未来にまつわるVirgilioの重大な警告を回想しながら、自らを待ち受ける運命を知ろうとする。

## Scene 28-36: The Divine Response
en: In *ella*, *Dante* expresses his longing to the mentioned *Beatrice*, and *Cacciaguida* responds in precise Latin, unlike the ambiguities that trapped *la gente folle* before the mentioned *l'Agnel di Dio*.
ja: その中で、DanteはBeatriceへの切なる憧憬を口にし、Cacciaguidaは正確なラテン語でそれに応えた。それは、l'Agnel di Dioの御前でla gente folleを惑わせたあの曖昧さとは無縁の、明晰な響きであった。

## Scene 37-45: The Theology of Contingency
en: In *ella*, *Cacciaguida* explains that human contingency is revealed in an eternal gaze without becoming necessity, and he informs *Dante* that the future awaiting him has now become visible to *Cacciaguida*.
ja: *ella*において、Cacciaguidaは、人間の偶然性は必然へと変じることなく永遠の眼差しのなかで顕現することを説き、Danteを待ち受ける未来が今やCacciaguidaに見えるようになったことを彼に伝えた。

## Scene 46-54: The Prediction of Exile
en: In *ella*, *Dante* is told that he must depart just as *Ipolito d'Atene* was forced to flee because of *Fedra*, while the *mercatanti e cittadini di Fiorenza* plot his exile in a place where *Cristo* is mentioned.
ja: その中でDanteは、Fedraのゆえに逃亡を強いられたIpolito d'Ateneのように、ここを去らねばならないと告げられる。同時に、Cristoの名が挙げられる場所では、mercatanti e cittadini di Fiorenzaが彼の追放を企んでいた。

## Scene 55-69: The Hardships of Exile
en: In *ella*, *Dante* is told he will lose all he loves and endure the bitterness of dependency, while the mentioned *la compagnia malvagia e scempia* is predicted to betray him before meeting their own ruin.
ja: 彼女の中で、Danteは愛するすべてを失い、依存という名の苦渋に耐える運命にあると告げられ、同時に、前述の la compagnia malvagia e scempia は、自らが破滅を迎える前に彼を裏切るであろうと予言された。

## Scene 70-75: The First Refuge
en: In *ella*, *Dante* is told that his first refuge will be the courtesy of the mentioned *Enrico VII*, who will offer aid before *Dante* even asks for it.
ja: *ella*の中で、Danteは、最初の寄る辺となるのは前述のEnrico VIIの厚意であり、Danteが助けを請うよりも先に、彼が助力を申し出てくれるだろうと告げられる。

## Scene 76-84: The Rise of the Virtuous Leader
en: In *ella*, *Dante* is told that *Arrigo* was born under a powerful star and possesses a virtue that *le genti* have not yet recognized, which will be evident before *'l Guasco* deceives him.
ja: 彼女の中で、Danteは、Arrigoが強き星の下に生まれ、le gentiが未だ気づかぬ徳を宿していることを告げられる。その徳は、'l Guascoが彼を欺く前に明らかになるという。

## Scene 85-93: The Global Impact of the Leader
en: In *ella*, *Cacciaguida* tells *Dante* that the magnificence of *Enrico VII* will be undeniable even to his *nemici* and that his benefits will transform *molta gente*, altering the fortunes of *ricchi e mendici*. He predicts this legacy will be so etched in the collective mind that *Dante* will not need to speak of it.
ja: 彼女の中で、CacciaguidaはDanteに、Enrico VIIの威光はnemiciにさえ否定できぬものであり、その恩恵はmolta genteを変容させ、ricchi e mendiciの運命をも塗り替えるだろうと説いた。この記憶は人々の心に深く刻まれるため、Danteがわざわざ語る必要はないだろうと、彼は予言した。

## Scene 94-99: A Warning Against Envy
en: In *ella*, *Cacciaguida* concludes his prophecy and warns *Dante* not to feel envy toward the mentioned *vicini*, as *Dante*'s life will extend far beyond the punishment of their perfidy.
ja: Cacciaguidaはそこで予言を締めくくり、前述のviciniを羨まないようDanteに警告する。Danteの生涯は、彼らの不忠に対する罰を遥かに超えて長く続くからである。

## Scene 100-105: Dante's Request for Counsel
en: In *ella*, *Dante*, longing and doubting, seeks counsel from *Cacciaguida*, who sees and loves rightly, as prompted by the mentioned *Beatrice*.
ja: そこにおいて、憧憬と疑念に揺れるDanteは、前述のBeatriceに促され、正しく見、正しく愛するCacciaguidaに助言を求める。

## Scene 106-120: Dante's Existential Fear
en: In *ella*, *Dante* tells *Cacciaguida* that he recognizes the impending blow of fate and must be prudent, as the truths he learned—during the journey where the mentioned *Beatrice* lifted him—will be bitter to many. He fears that these revelations may cost him his place among the mentioned *coloro che questo tempo chiameranno antico*.
ja: そこでDanteはCacciaguidaに、差し迫った運命の打撃を悟り、慎重にならねばならないと告げる。Beatriceに引き上げられた旅路で得た真理は多くの人々にとって苦いものとなるはずであり、彼はその啓示によって、coloro che questo tempo chiameranno anticoの間における自らの居場所を失うことを危惧していた。

## Scene 121-135: The Necessity of Bitter Truth
en: In *ella*, *Beatrice* encourages *Dante* to speak the truth without fear, explaining that while his blunt words may provoke the guilty, they will ultimately provide vital nourishment. She tells him that his cry will strike the highest peaks like a wind, and that such an effect is a matter of honor.
ja: BeatriceはDanteに対し、恐れることなく真実を語るよう促し、その率直な言葉がたとえ罪人を刺激したとしても、最終的には不可欠な糧となるのだと説いた。彼の叫びは風のように最高峰を打つはずであり、そのような影響を及ぼすことこそが名誉であると、彼女は告げた。

## Scene 136-142: The Purpose of the Vision
en: In `*ella*`, `*Dante*` is told that the `*anime*` were shown to him so that `*quel ch'ode*` may have a firm faith based on clear examples.
ja: そこでは、`quel ch'ode`が明白な実例に基づいた確固たる信仰を持てるように、`anime`が`Dante`に示されたのだと告げられた。
