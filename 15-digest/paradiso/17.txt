# Canto 17 — The Prophecy of Exile and the Vision of the Great Lombard

## Scene 1-6: The Mythological Parallel
en: In *ella*, *Dante* compares his state to that of *Faetone*, who sought *Climenè* to confirm his identity, a pursuit that often separates *li padri* from *ai figli*. This restlessness was perceived by *Beatrice*.
ja: ellaにおいて、Danteは自らの境遇を、正体を確かめるべくClimenèを追い求め、しばしばli padriをai figliから引き離すことになるFaetoneの姿に重ね合わせた。こうした心の揺らぎを、Beatriceは見抜いていた。

## Scene 7-12: Beatrice's Instruction
en: In *ella*, *Beatrice* instructs *Dante* to voice his desire aloud, not to increase her own knowledge, but so that he may receive an answer from the mentioned *Cacciaguida*.
ja: BeatriceはDanteに、自らの望みを声に出して語るよう促した。それは彼女自身の知識を増やすためではなく、先述のCacciaguidaから答えを得るためであった。

## Scene 13-27: Dante's Plea for Knowledge
en: In *ella*, *Dante* speaks of *Beatrice* and her divine perspective, which transcends that of *le terrene menti*, recalling that *Virgilio* had warned him of his future while he was with the *anime*. He expresses his desire to know the fate that awaits him, believing that anticipation makes the blow easier to bear.
ja: DanteはBeatriceについて、そしてle terrene mentiを凌駕する彼女の神聖な視座について語り、animeと共にいた時にVirgilioが自身の未来について警告したことを思い起こす。あらかじめ予期していれば衝撃も和らぐはずだと信じ、彼は自らを待ち受ける運命を知りたいと願う。

## Scene 28-36: The Divine Response
en: In *ella*, *Dante* confesses his desire as *Beatrice* had directed, and *Cacciaguida* responds in precise Latin, avoiding the ambiguities that once misled *la gente folle* before the coming of *l'Agnel di Dio*.
ja: 彼女の中で、DanteはBeatriceに指示された通りに自らの切なる願いを告白し、Cacciaguidaは、l'Agnel di Dioの降臨以前にla gente folleを惑わせた曖昧さを排した厳格なラテン語をもって、それに応えた。

## Scene 37-45: The Theology of Contingency
en: In *ella*, *Cacciaguida* explains how human contingency is known to the eternal gaze without becoming necessity, revealing that he can now see the future preparing itself for *Dante*.
ja: その中でCacciaguidaは、人間の偶発性が必然へと変わることなく永遠なるまなざしに知られていることを説き、Danteに待ち受ける未来が今や自分に見えていることを明かす。

## Scene 46-54: The Prediction of Exile
en: In *ella*, *Dante* is told he must depart just as *Ipolito d'Atene* once fled from *Fedra*, while the *mercatanti e cittadini di Fiorenza* plot his exile in a place where *Cristo* is mentioned.
ja: そこにてDanteは、かつてIpolito d'AteneがFedraから逃れたように自らも立ち去らねばならぬと告げられ、その頃Cristoの名が唱えられる場所では、mercatanti e cittadini di Fiorenzaが彼の追放を企んでいた。

## Scene 55-69: The Hardships of Exile
en: In *ella*, *Dante* is warned that he will lose all he loves and endure the humiliation of dependence, as well as the betrayal of the mentioned *la compagnia malvagia e scempia*, whose own wickedness will ultimately prove their undoing.
ja: ellaの中で、Danteは、愛するすべてを失い、依存という屈辱に耐え、さらには先に触れたla compagnia malvagia e scempiaに裏切られることを警告される。しかし、彼らの邪悪さは、最終的に彼ら自身の破滅を招くことになる。

## Scene 70-75: The First Refuge
en: In *ella*, *Dante* is told that his first refuge will be the courtesy of the mentioned *Enrico VII*, who will show such proactive kindness that the offer of aid will precede the request.
ja: 彼女はDanteに、最初の拠り所となるのは前述のEnrico VIIの厚意であると告げた。その親切心は至極能動的なものであり、助けを請うよりも先に援助の申し出があるという。

## Scene 76-84: The Rise of the Virtuous Leader
en: In *ella*, *Dante* is told that he will encounter *Arrigo*, a man born under a fortunate star whose virtues will be evident before *'l Guasco* betrays him, though *le genti* are not yet aware of his significance.
ja: そこでDanteは、幸運な星の下に生まれたArrigoに巡り合うことを告げられる。'l Guascoに裏切られる前にその美徳は明白となるはずだが、le gentiはまだ彼の重要性に気づいていない。

## Scene 85-93: The Global Impact of the Leader
en: In `*ella*`, `*Cacciaguida*` informs `*Dante*` that `*Enrico VII*` awaits him with benefits and a glory that even `*nemici*` cannot deny. He predicts that `*Enrico VII*` will transform `*molta gente*`, altering the fates of `*ricchi e mendici*` so profoundly that `*Dante*` will not need to speak of it.
ja: そこで`Cacciaguida`は`Dante`に、`Enrico VII`が`nemici`ですら否定し得ない恩恵と栄光をもって彼を待ち受けていると告げた。さらに、`Enrico VII`が`molta gente`を変貌させ、`ricchi e mendici`の運命をあまりに深く変えるため、`Dante`がそれを語る必要さえなくなるだろうと予言した。

## Scene 94-99: A Warning Against Envy
en: In *ella*, *Cacciaguida* concludes his warning, urging *Dante* not to feel envy toward the mentioned *vicini*, as *Dante*'s life will endure far beyond the punishment of their perfidies.
ja: Cacciaguidaはその中で警告を締めくくり、Danteに、先述のviciniに対して羨望を抱かぬよう説いた。彼らの不実への罰を遥かに超えて、Danteの生は永く続いていくからである。

## Scene 100-105: Dante's Request for Counsel
en: In *ella*, prompted by the mentioned *Beatrice*, *Dante* seeks counsel from *Cacciaguida*, longing for the guidance of one who sees and loves rightly.
ja: その中で、Beatriceに促されたDanteは、正しく見、正しく愛する者の導きを切望して、Cacciaguidaに助言を求める。

## Scene 106-120: Dante's Existential Fear
en: In `*ella*`, `*Dante*` tells `*Cacciaguida*` that he sees his misfortune approaching and must act with prudence so that his writings do not leave him without refuge. Recalling the guidance of `*Beatrice*`, he fears that the bitter truths he reveals will make him a stranger even to those who follow.
ja: `Dante`は`Cacciaguida`に、自らに不幸が忍び寄っているのが見え、自身の著作が逃げ場を奪うことにならぬよう慎重に振る舞わねばならないと語る。`Beatrice`の導きを思い返しながら、彼は、自らが明かす苦い真実によって、後世の人々にさえ疎まれた存在となるのではないかと危惧している。

## Scene 121-135: The Necessity of Bitter Truth
en: In `*ella*`, *Beatrice* reassures *Dante* that while his blunt words will provoke those with guilty consciences, they will eventually serve as vital nourishment and bring him honor by striking the highest peaks.
ja: `ella`において、BeatriceはDanteに言い聞かせた。彼の率直な言葉は、罪悪感を抱く者たちを刺激するだろうが、やがては不可欠な糧となり、最高峰に達することで彼に名誉をもたらすだろう、と。

## Scene 136-142: The Purpose of the Vision
en: In *ella*, *Dante* is told that the *anime* were shown to him so that the listener's faith would be rooted in clear examples rather than hidden proofs.
ja: その中でDanteは、聞き手の信仰が秘められた証明ではなく、明白な例に根ざすように、animeたちが彼に示されたのだと告げられた。
