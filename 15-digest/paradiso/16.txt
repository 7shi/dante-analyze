# Canto 16 — Cacciaguida's Prophecy and the Lament for Florence

## Scene 1-12: Reflections on Lineage and Time
en: *Cacciaguida* argues that it is pointless for the `*gente*` to boast of nobility *qua giù*, as such prestige shrinks unless actively preserved. He turns his thoughts to `*voi che prima a Roma s'offerie*` and `*la sua famiglia*`.
ja: Cacciaguidaは、絶えず維持されなければ衰えゆくものであるため、`gente` が地上で高貴さを誇ることは無意味であると説く。そして、彼は `voi che prima a Roma s'offerie` と `la sua famiglia` に思いを馳せた。

## Scene 13-15: Beatrice's Laughter
en: Standing slightly apart at *qua giù* in the region of *ella*, *Beatrice* laughs, appearing as the mentioned *Ginevra* did at her first written fault.
ja: qua giùのellaの領域で、少し離れて佇むBeatriceが微笑んでいる。その姿は、前述のGinevraが最初の書き記された過ちにおいて見せた様子に似ていた。

## Scene 16-27: Dante's Inquiry
en: *Dante*, emboldened and joyful in the presence of *Cacciaguida* in *qua giù* within *ella*, asks him about *li vostri antichi*, the state of *San Giovanni*, and which of *le genti* were most distinguished in those years.
ja: Danteは、この下の地でCacciaguidaと対面し、勇気づけられ、歓びに満ちて、li vostri antichiのことやSan Giovanniの様子、そして当時のle gentiの中で誰が最も名高かったのかを彼に尋ねた。

## Scene 28-39: Cacciaguida's Origins
en: In *qua giù* within *ella*, *Cacciaguida* brightens like a fanned coal in response to *Dante* and speaks in a sweet, archaic voice. He recounts the years that have passed since he was born to *mia madre*.
ja: ここ下のその内で、CacciaguidaはDanteに応えて煽られた炭のように輝き、心地よく古風な声で語りかける。彼はmia madreのもとに生まれてから過ぎ去った歳月を回想する。

## Scene 40-48: The Ancestral Boundaries
en: In `*qua giù*` within `*ella*`, `*Cacciaguida*` relates the birth of himself and `*Li antichi miei*` to `*quei che corre il vostro annüal gioco*`, while opting for silence regarding the specifics of `*Li antichi miei*`. He notes that `*color ch'a quel tempo eran ivi*` capable of bearing arms by `*l Batista*` were only a fifth of `*quei ch'or son vivi*`.
ja: その領域の下界において、`Cacciaguida`は自身と`Li antichi miei`の誕生について`quei che corre il vostro annüal gioco`に語るが、`Li antichi miei`の詳細についてはあえて沈黙を守った。彼は、`l Batista`の時代に武装できた`color ch'a quel tempo eran ivi`は、`quei ch'or son vivi`のわずか五分の一に過ぎなかったと述べている。

## Scene 49-57: The Dilution of Citizenship
en: In `*qua giù*` within `*ella*`, `*Cacciaguida*` laments that purity was only visible in the `*ultimo artista*` and argues that it would be better if `*quelle genti*` had remained as neighbors. He expresses disgust at enduring the stench of the `*villan d'Aguglion*` and `*quel da Signa*`.
ja: この下界の街において、`Cacciaguida`は純潔がただ`ultimo artista`にのみ宿っていたことを嘆き、むしろ`quelle genti`が隣人として留まってくれていた方がましだったと説く。そして、`villan d'Aguglion`や`quel da Signa`が放つ悪臭に耐え忍ぶことに、激しい嫌悪を露わにした。

## Scene 58-66: The Loss of Florentine Virtue
en: In `*qua giù*` within `*ella*`, the `*gente*` are spoken of as being so aberrant that, had they been a kind mother to the `*figlio*` of `*Cesare*` rather than a stepmother, `*l'avolo*` would have sought his lands and the `*Conti*`, `*Cerchi*`, and `*Buondelmonti*` would have remained in their ancestral estates.
ja: 彼女の中のこの地に住まうgenteはあまりに常軌を逸しており、もし彼らがCesareのfiglioにとって継母ではなく慈しみ深い母であったなら、l'avoloは自らの領地を求め、Conti、Cerchi、そしてBuondelmontiもまた先祖代々の地にとどまっていたであろうと言われている。

## Scene 67-78: The Instability of Cities
en: In `*qua giù*` within `*ella*`, `*Dante*` is told that the confusion of `*persone*` is the root of a city's ruin and that the undoing of `*le schiatte*` is a natural fate.
ja: その街の下界にて、`Dante`は、`persone`の不和こそが都市を破滅へと導く根源であり、`le schiatte`の没落は避けがたい宿命であると告げられた。

## Scene 79-87: The Unpredictability of Fortune
en: In *qua giù* within *ella*, *Cacciaguida* explains to *Dante* that all human achievements are mortal and fluctuate like the tide. He asserts that it is therefore not surprising that he will speak of the lost fame of *li alti Fiorentini*.
ja: CacciaguidaはDanteに対し、この世における人のあらゆる業績は儚く、潮の満ち引きのように移ろうものであると説いた。それゆえ、li alti Fiorentiniの失われた名声について語ることは、何ら不思議ではないと彼は断言した。

## Scene 88-93: The Catalog of Illustrious Families
en: In *qua giù* within *ella*, *Cacciaguida* describes seeing *li Ughi*, *i Catellini*, *i Filippi*, *i Greci*, *Ormanni*, and *Alberichi* already in decline. He further recalls the enduring prestige of *quel de la Sannella* and *la Sannella*, *quel de l'Arca*, *Soldanieri*, *Ardinghi*, and *Bostichi*.
ja: Cacciaguidaはこの街の内で、li Ughi, i Catellini, i Filippi, i Greci, Ormanni, そして Alberichiがすでに没落しつつある様を語る。あわせて彼は、quel de la Sannellaとla Sannella, quel de l'Arca, Soldanieri, Ardinghi, そして Bostichiが保ち続ける不変の威信を回想するのである。

## Scene 94-108: The Rise of Corrupt Families
en: In `*qua giù*` within `*ella*`, a warning is issued that new treachery threatens to sink the city, and the former standing of `*conte Guido*`, `*Bellincione*`, `*Quel de la Pressa*`, `*Galigaio*`, `*Vaio*`, `*Sacchetti*`, `*Giuochi*`, `*Fifanti*`, `*Barucci*`, `*Galli*`, `*quei ch'arrossan per lo staio*`, `*Calfucci*`, `*Sizii*`, and `*Arrigucci*` is recounted.
ja: 都市の内のこの地において、新たな裏切りが街を沈めようとしているという警告が発せられ、`conte Guido`、`Bellincione`、`Quel de la Pressa`、`Galigaio`、`Vaio`、`Sacchetti`、`Giuochi`、`Fifanti`、`Barucci`、`Galli`、`quei ch'arrossan per lo staio`、`Calfucci`、`Sizii`、そして`Arrigucci`らのかつての地位が語られる。

## Scene 109-118: The Pride of the New Class
en: In `*qua giù*` within `*ella*`, `*Cacciaguida*` speaks of `*quei che son disfatti per lor superbia*` and the `*i padri*` of `*coloro che si fanno grassi stando a consistoro*`. He further denounces the opportunistic nature and lowly origins of `*l'oltracotata schiatta*`.
ja: 天上の圏域から望むこの地上において、`Cacciaguida`は`quei che son disfatti per lor superbia`や、`coloro che si fanno grassi stando a consistoro`の`i padri`について語った。さらに彼は、`l'oltracotata schiatta`の卑しき出自と、その日和見主義的な性質を激しく糾弾した。

## Scene 119-126: The Shifts in the Marketplace
en: In *qua giù* within *ella*, *Cacciaguida* describes how *Ubertin Donato* resented the familial ties created by his *suocero* and notes the arrival of *'l Caponsacco* and the so-called *buon cittadino*, *Giuda* and *Infangato*, who entered through the gate of *quei de la Pera*.
ja: ここ下方の街において、CacciaguidaはUbertin Donatoがsuoceroによって結ばれた親族の絆にいかに憤っていたかを語り、'l Caponsaccoや、いわゆるbuon cittadino、そしてGiudaとInfangatoがquei de la Peraの門から入ってきたことに言及している。

## Scene 127-135: The Loss of Privileges
en: In `*qua giù*` within `*ella*`, it is recounted that `*Ciascun che de la bella insegna porta*` once derived privilege from the `*gran barone*`, whose prestige is linked to `*Tommaso*`, but now `*colui che la fascia col fregio*` mingles with others. The `*Gualterotti*` and `*Importuni*` are mentioned alongside a lament for the peace disrupted by `*novi vicin*`.
ja: その地の下方において、かつて`Ciascun che de la bella insegna porta`が`Tommaso`に連なる威信を持つ`gran barone`から特権を授かっていたことが語られるが、今や`colui che la fascia col fregio`は他の者たちと混じり合っている。そこには`Gualterotti`や`Importuni`の名と共に、`novi vicin`によって乱された平穏への嘆きが綴られている。

## Scene 136-144: The Tragedy of Buondelmonte
en: In *qua giù* within *ella*, *Dante* is told that the honor of *Buondelmonte* and *suoi consorti* was lost after *Buondelmonte* avoided his wedding to *Ema*. It is lamented that if *Dio* had granted this union upon *Buondelmonte*'s first arrival, the resulting sorrows would have been avoided.
ja: この地の下にて、Danteは、BuondelmonteがEmaとの婚礼を避けたために、Buondelmonteとsuoi consortiの名誉が失われたことを告げられる。もしBuondelmonteが最初に訪れた際にDioがこの結びつきを許していたなら、その後の悲しみは避けられたはずだと、そこでは嘆かれていた。

## Scene 145-154: The Vision of a Lost Peace
en: In `*qua giù*` within `*ella*`, `*Cacciaguida*` recalls a lost era of peace involving `*queste genti*` and describes how `*il popol suo*` was once glorious and just.
ja: その地のここ下界において、Cacciaguidaはqueste gentiにまつわる失われた平和な時代を回想し、il popol suoがいかにかつて栄光に満ち、公正であったかを語る。
