# Canto 31 — The Vision of the Celestial Rose and the Guidance of Saint Bernard

## Scene 1-12: The Celestial Rose and the Angelic Bees
en: *Dante* observes *la milizia santa*, whom *Cristo* made a bride, appearing as a *candida rosa*. *Beatrice* flies like a bee, descending into this rose and then returning to *Dio*.
ja: Danteは、Cristoが花嫁として迎えたla milizia santaが白い薔薇となって現れるのを眺めていた。Beatriceは蜂のごとくその薔薇へと舞い降り、そして再びDioのもとへと戻っていく。

## Scene 13-24: The Splendor of Divine Light
en: *Tanta moltitudine volante* fly between the light and the *candida rosa*, distributing the peace and ardor they acquire. Their immense numbers do not obstruct the light, which penetrates all things.
ja: *tanta moltitudine volante* が光と白い薔薇の間を飛び交い、自らが得た平安と情熱を振りまいている。その数は膨大であるが、万物を貫く光を遮ることはない。

## Scene 25-27: The Nature of the Eternal Kingdom
en: This secure and joyful *regno* is filled with *gente antica e in novella*, whose vision and love are all focused upon a single point.
ja: 安らぎと喜びに満ちたこの王国には、gente antica e in novellaが満ち、その視線と愛はすべて、ただ一点へと注がれている。

## Scene 28-42: Dante's Prayer to the Trinity
en: In the *regno* *qua sù*, *Dante* prays to the light that satisfies *i beati*, comparing his own profound awe to the imagined astonishment of *i barbari* and recalling a *popol giusto e sano*.
ja: この上の王国にて、Danteはi beatiを満たす光に祈りを捧げ、自らの深き畏怖をi barbariが抱くであろう驚きになぞらえながら、popol giusto e sanoに思いを馳せた。

## Scene 43-54: The Wandering Gaze
en: *Dante* lets his gaze wander through the *regno* *qua sù*, observing the faces of *altri* filled with light and charity. He grasps the general form of the place, though he has not yet fixed his eyes on any one person.
ja: Danteは視線をこの上の王国へと走らせ、光と慈愛に満ちたaltriの面々を眺めた。まだ特定の誰かに目を留めたわけではないが、彼はその場所の概貌を捉えていた。

## Scene 55-63: The Appearance of Saint Bernard
en: *Dante* asks *Dio* to turn him back toward *Beatrice* for further questions, but he perceives *San Bernardo* instead. Dressed as *le genti glorïose* in the *regno*, *San Bernardo* looks upon him with a benign, paternal joy.
ja: Danteはさらなる問いを重ねるため、再びBeatriceの方へ向かせてもらうようDioに願ったが、代わりに彼の目に現れたのはSan Bernardoであった。その領域のle genti glorïoseとしての装いに身を包んだSan Bernardoは、慈愛に満ちた父のような喜びをもって彼を見つめていた。

## Scene 64-69: The Location of Beatrice
en: *Dante* asks *San Bernardo* where *Beatrice* is, and *San Bernardo* explains that *Beatrice* moved him from his position to fulfill *Dante*'s desire, directing him to look for her in the *regno* *qua sù*.
ja: DanteがSan BernardoにBeatriceの居場所を尋ねると、San Bernardoは、Danteの願いを叶えるためにBeatriceが自分をその場から動かしたのだと説明し、この上の王国で彼女を探すようにと彼に促した。

## Scene 70-78: A Distant Glimpse of Beatrice
en: *Dante* looks up in the *regno* *qua sù* and sees *Beatrice* as a crown reflecting eternal rays, though she is as distant as the heights are to *occhio mortale alcun* or as the surface is to *qualunque* who dives into the sea. Consequently, her image does not clearly reach him.
ja: Danteが天上の王国を仰ぎ見ると、そこには永遠の光を反射する冠のようなBeatriceの姿があった。しかし彼女は、occhio mortale alcunにとっての頂や、海に潜るqualunqueにとっての海面のごとく遥か遠くにいたため、その面影は彼に鮮明には届かなかった。

## Scene 79-90: Dante's Address to Beatrice
en: In the *regno* *qua sù*, *Dante* addresses *Beatrice*, thanking her for the grace and sacrifice that led him to spiritual freedom. He prays that his soul, made whole by her, may eventually detach from his body to dwell in her presence.
ja: regno qua sùにて、DanteはBeatriceに語りかけ、自らを精神的な自由へと導いた彼女の恩寵と犠牲に感謝を捧げる。彼女によって完全となった自らの魂が、やがて肉体を離れ、彼女の御許に憩うことができるよう、彼は祈る。

## Scene 91-93: Beatrice's Silent Response
en: In the *regno* *qua sù*, *Dante* finishes his prayer, and *Beatrice*, though distant, smiles and glances back at him before returning to the light.
ja: 天上の王国において、Danteが祈りを終えると、遠方にいたBeatriceが、光の中へ戻る前に微笑んで彼を振り返った。

## Scene 94-102: Bernard's Instruction
en: *San Bernardo* instructs *Dante* to survey *questo giardino* to prepare his sight for the divine ascent, assuring him that *Maria* will grant him every grace because of *San Bernardo*'s fidelity.
ja: San Bernardoは、神聖なる上昇に備えて視界を整えるようDanteにこの庭を眺めることを勧め、自らの忠誠ゆえにMariaがあらゆる恩寵を授けてくれるだろうと彼に請け合った。

## Scene 103-111: Dante's Internal Wonder
en: In *questo giardino*, *Dante* gazes upon the living charity of *San Bernardo* with a profound wonder comparable to beholding the image of *Cristo*. He admires the peace that *San Bernardo* had already tasted through contemplation during his earthly life.
ja: この庭において、DanteはSan Bernardoの生ける慈愛を、あたかもCristoの姿を仰ぎ見るかのような深い驚嘆をもって見つめていた。彼は、San Bernardoが地上の生において観照を通じてすでに味わっていた平安に、深く感銘を受けていた。

## Scene 112-117: The Command to Look Higher
en: *San Bernardo* tells *Dante* that the joy of *questo giardino* will remain unknown if he keeps his gaze at the bottom, and urges him to look instead toward the most distant circles to see *Maria*, the queen of this realm.
ja: San BernardoはDanteに、視線を下に向けたままではquesto giardinoの喜びを知ることはないだろうと告げ、この領域の女王であるMariaを仰ぎ見るため、視線を最も遠き円へと移すよう促した。

## Scene 118-129: The Shifting Light of the Heavens
en: In *questo giardino* *qua sù*, *Dante* lifts his gaze and sees the light intensify from the lower reaches to the highest, like the dawn of a morning sky. This peaceful radiance reaches its peak in the center and fades equally toward every edge.
ja: この上の*questo giardino*でDanteが視線を上げると、朝焼けの空のように下から上へと強まりゆく光が見えた。その穏やかな輝きは中心で頂点に達し、あらゆる縁に向かって等しく淡く消えていく。

## Scene 130-138: The Vision of the Queen's Joy
en: In `*questo giardino*`, `*Dante*` observes `*mille angeli festanti*` and beholds `*Maria*` laughing, her joy reflected in the eyes of `*tutti li altri santi*`. He acknowledges that he lacks the power to describe even the smallest part of her delight.
ja: この庭で、Danteはmille angeli festantiを眺め、tutti li altri santiの瞳に喜びを映して微笑むMariaを仰ぎ見る。彼は、彼女の歓喜のほんの一端さえも描写する力が自分にはないことを認めた。

## Scene 139-142: Bernard's Shared Devotion
en: In `*questo giardino*` `*qua sù*`, `*San Bernardo*` notices the narrator's focused gaze and turns his own eyes with affection toward `*Maria*`, intensifying the narrator's own ardent vision.
ja: ここ上のこの庭において、San Bernardoは語り手の注がれる眼差しに気づき、慈しみを込めてMariaへと視線を転じた。そのさまが、語り手の熱烈な視線をいっそう激しくさせた。
