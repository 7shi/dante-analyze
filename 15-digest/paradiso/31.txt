# Canto 31 — The Vision of the Celestial Rose and the Guidance of Saint Bernard

## Scene 1-12: The Celestial Rose and the Angelic Bees
en: In the *candida rosa*, *Dante* sees *la milizia santa*, whom *Cristo* made a bride. Like a bee, *Beatrice* flies between the rose and *Dio*, descending into the flower and then ascending back to the source of her love.
ja: 白い薔薇の中で、DanteはCristoが花嫁としたla milizia santaを目の当たりにする。Beatriceは一匹の蜂のように薔薇とDioの間を飛び交い、花の中へと舞い降りては、再び自らの愛の源へと昇りゆく。

## Scene 13-24: The Splendor of Divine Light
en: In the *candida rosa*, *tanta moltitudine volante* with flaming faces and golden wings distribute peace and fervor as they fly. Despite their vast numbers, *tanta moltitudine volante* do not obstruct the divine light that penetrates all things.
ja: 白い薔薇の中で、炎のごとき貌と黄金の翼を持つtanta moltitudine volanteが、飛翔しながら平安と熱情を振りまいている。夥しい数であるにもかかわらず、tanta moltitudine volanteは、万物を貫く神聖な光を遮ることはないのである。

## Scene 25-27: The Nature of the Eternal Kingdom
en: In this secure and joyful *regno*, the *gente antica e in novella* all direct their vision and love toward a single point.
ja: 安寧と歓喜に満ちたこの王国において、gente antica e in novellaは皆、ただ一点に視線と愛を注いでいる。

## Scene 28-42: Dante's Prayer to the Trinity
en: In the *regno*, *Dante* prays to the light that satisfies *i beati*, comparing his own awe to the amazement of *i barbari* and thinking of the *popol giusto e sano*.
ja: その王国において、Danteはi beatiを満たす光に祈りを捧げ、自らの畏怖をi barbariの驚愕になぞらえつつ、popol giusto e sanoに思いを馳せた。

## Scene 43-54: The Wandering Gaze
en: *Dante* scans the ranks of the *regno* like a pilgrim in a temple, observing the luminous and charitable faces of *altri*. He takes in the general form of the *regno* without yet fixing his gaze on any one of them.
ja: Danteは寺院の巡礼者のように王国の列を眺め、altriの光り輝く慈愛に満ちた顔々に目を向けた。まだ誰か一人に視線を定めることなく、彼は王国の全体的な佇まいを捉えていた。

## Scene 55-63: The Appearance of Saint Bernard
en: In the *regno*, *Dante* asks *Dio* to turn him back toward *Beatrice*, but he discovers that the figure responding to him is actually *Bernardo*. Dressed like *le genti glorïose*, *Bernardo* gazes at him with a benign, paternal joy.
ja: その王国にて、DanteはDioにBeatriceの方へ戻らせてほしいと願うが、そこで応じた者が実はBernardoであることに気づく。le genti glorïoseのような装いに身を包んだBernardoは、慈愛に満ちた父のような喜びをもって彼を見つめていた。

## Scene 64-69: The Location of Beatrice
en: *Qua sù* in the *regno*, *Dante* asks *Bernardo* where *Beatrice* is, and *Bernardo* explains that *Beatrice* moved him to satisfy *Dante*'s desire, directing him to look up toward the third circle to see *Beatrice* in her throne.
ja: ここ王国の高みにて、DanteがBeatriceの行方を尋ねると、Bernardoは、Danteの望みを叶えるためにBeatriceが自分を動かしたのだと説明し、第3円の上の玉座に座るBeatriceを仰ぎ見るよう彼に促した。

## Scene 70-78: A Distant Glimpse of Beatrice
en: *Qua sù* in the *regno*, *Dante* looks up and sees *Beatrice* as a crown reflecting eternal rays, though she is as distant from him as the heights are from *occhio mortale alcun* or the deep sea is from *qualunque*. Due to this distance, her image does not descend to him.
ja: 王国のこの高みにて、Danteが仰ぎ見ると、Beatriceが永遠の光を反射する冠のように見えた。しかし彼女は、高みとocchio mortale alcunが、あるいは深海とqualunqueが隔てられているように遥か遠くにあり、その隔たりゆえに彼女の姿が彼のもとへ降りてくることはなかった。

## Scene 79-90: Dante's Address to Beatrice
en: *Qua sù* in the *regno*, *Dante* addresses *Beatrice* to thank her for the grace and virtue she used to lead him to spiritual freedom. He prays that *Beatrice*'s magnificence remains in him so that his soul may one day detach from his body to be pleasing to her.
ja: 天上の王国にて、DanteはBeatriceに語りかけ、自らを精神的な自由へと導いてくれた彼女の恩寵と徳に感謝を捧げた。そして、Beatriceの気高さが自らの内に留まり、いつの日か魂が肉体を離れて彼女に喜ばれる存在となれるよう、彼は祈った。

## Scene 91-93: Beatrice's Silent Response
en: *Qua sù* in the *regno*, *Dante* finishes his prayer, and *Beatrice*, despite her distance, smiles and glances back at him before returning to the eternal fountain.
ja: ここ天上界において、Danteは祈りを終えた。Beatriceは遠く離れていながらも、微笑んで彼を振り返ると、永遠の泉へと戻っていった。

## Scene 94-102: Bernard's Instruction
en: *Qua sù* in *questo giardino*, *Bernardo* instructs *Dante* to survey the surroundings to better prepare his sight for the divine rays, assuring him that *Maria* will grant him grace due to *Bernardo*'s faithfulness.
ja: この庭園にて、BernardoはDanteに、神聖なる光に目を慣らすため周囲を見渡すよう諭し、自らの忠実さゆえにMariaが彼に恩寵を授けるであろうことを約束した。

## Scene 103-111: Dante's Internal Wonder
en: In `*questo giardino*` `*qua sù*`, `*Dante*` gazes upon the living charity of `*Bernardo*` with a wonder similar to that of `*colui che forse di Croazia*` beholding the likeness of `*Iesù Cristo*`.
ja: ここ上のこの庭において、DanteはBernardoの生ける慈愛を、あたかもcolui che forse di CroaziaがIesù Cristoの御姿を仰ぎ見たときのような驚嘆をもって見つめていた。

## Scene 112-117: The Command to Look Higher
en: In *questo giardino* *qua sù*, *Bernardo* tells *Dante* that he will not understand the joy of the place if he keeps his gaze low, but must instead look toward the most distant circles to behold *Maria*.
ja: *questo giardino qua sù*にて、BernardoはDanteに、視線を低く留めていてはここにある歓喜を理解できぬだろうと説いた。Mariaを仰ぎ見るためには、最も遠い円へと目を向けなければならないのだ。

## Scene 118-129: The Shifting Light of the Heavens
en: In `*questo giardino*` `*qua sù*`, `*Dante*` raises his gaze to observe how the divine light intensifies as he looks higher and how it radiates most vividly from the center, fading equally toward the edges.
ja: この上の庭で、Danteは視線を上げ、高く仰ぎ見るほどに神々しい光が強まり、中心から最も鮮烈に放たれたその輝きが、縁に向かって等しく淡くなっていく様を見つめた。

## Scene 130-138: The Vision of the Queen's Joy
en: In *questo giardino* *qua sù*, *Dante* beholds *mille angeli festanti* and the laughter of *Maria*, whose joy is mirrored in the eyes of *tutti li altri santi*. *Dante* admits that his words cannot capture the magnitude of *Maria*'s delight.
ja: ここ高き庭にて、Danteはmille angeli festantiとMariaの笑い声を目の当たりにし、その喜びはtutti li altri santiの瞳にも鏡のように映し出されていた。Mariaが湛える歓喜のあまりの大きさを前に、Danteは自らの言葉では到底捉えきれないことを認めた。

## Scene 139-142: Bernard's Shared Devotion
en: In `*questo giardino*` `*qua sù*`, `*Bernardo*` observes the narrator's attentive gaze and turns his own eyes with affection toward `*Maria*`, which further inflames the narrator's fervor.
ja: この上の庭で、Bernardoは語り手の熱い視線に気づき、慈しむようにMariaへと目を向けた。その光景が、語り手の情熱をいっそう激しく燃え上がらせた。
