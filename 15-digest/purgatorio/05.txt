# Canto 05 — The Interruption of the Souls and the Three Testimonies

## Scene 1-6: The Sudden Cry
en: *Dante* had already departed from *quell' ombre* and was following *Virgilio* when, *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *una* cried out and pointed, noting that *Dante* appeared to be alive.
ja: Danteはすでにquell' ombreを離れ、Virgilioの後についていたが、そのとき、岩の陰からunaが叫んで指をさし、Danteは生きているように見えると告げた。

## Scene 7-9: Dante's Distraction
en: At the sound of the words, *Dante* turns *a l'ombra dietro al sasso* and sees that he is being gazed upon in wonder.
ja: その言葉を聞いてDanteが岩の背後の影の方を振り返ると、驚きをもって自分が見つめられていることに気づいた。

## Scene 10-18: Virgil's Admonishment
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Virgilio* urges *Dante* to ignore *le genti* and remain as steadfast as a tower. He warns that any *l'omo* who allows distracting thoughts to proliferate will lose sight of his goal.
ja: 岩の背後の影にて、VirgilioはDanteにle gentiを無視し、塔のごとく揺るぎなくあれと促した。雑念を増殖させるl'omoは、自らの目的を見失うことになると彼は警告した。

## Scene 19-21: Dante's Compliance
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Dante* tells *Virgilio* that he is following, blushing with the color that sometimes makes *l'uom* worthy of pardon.
ja: 岩の背後の影にて、DanteはVirgilioに自分は後に続いていると告げた。その頬は、時としてl'uomを赦されるに足るものとするあの色に染まっていた。

## Scene 22-27: The Approaching Procession
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *le genti* pass before *Dante* and *Virgilio* singing the "Miserere," but they suddenly cry out in surprise when they realize that light cannot pass through the physical body of *Dante*.
ja: 岩の背後の影で、Miserereを唱えながらle gentiがDanteとVirgilioの前を通り過ぎるが、Danteの肉体が光を透過させないことに気づいた彼らは、不意に驚愕の声を上げた。

## Scene 28-30: The Messengers Arrive
en: Two of the *anime*, acting as messengers, run toward *Dante* and *Virgilio* at *a l'ombra dietro al sasso* and ask them to explain their condition.
ja: 使者の役割を担った2体のanimeが、a l'ombra dietro al sassoにてDanteとVirgilioのもとへ駆け寄り、彼らにその境遇を説明するよう求めた。

## Scene 31-36: Virgil's Verification
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Virgilio* tells the *anime* to return to those who sent them and confirm that *Dante* is made of true flesh. He adds that if they paused to see his shadow, this answer is sufficient and they should honor him.
ja: 岩の背後の陰で、Virgilioはanimeたちに、自分たちを遣わした者たちの元へ戻り、Danteが真の肉体を持つ人間であることを伝えよと告げた。そして、もし彼らが彼の影を見て足を止めたのであれば、それだけで十分な答えとなるはずであり、彼を敬うべきであると付け加えた。

## Scene 37-42: The Swarming Souls
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Dante* and *Virgilio* observe the mentioned *schiera* rushing toward them like an unrestrained crowd.
ja: 岩の背後の陰で、DanteとVirgilioは、制御不能な群衆のように自分たちへと押し寄せてくる例のschieraを眺めていた。

## Scene 43-45: The Poet's Warning
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Virgilio* tells *Dante* that the large crowd of *questa gente* is pressing in to beseech him, and he instructs *Dante* to keep moving while listening to them.
ja: 岩の背後の陰にて、Virgilioは、questa genteの大群が自分に懇願しようと押し寄せていることをDanteに告げ、彼らの訴えを聞きながらも、先へ進むよう指示した。

## Scene 46-57: The Collective Plea
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *i peccatori* beseech *Dante* to linger and see if he recognizes *alcun* among them to whom he might bring news. They reveal that they were sinners who repented through the light of *Dio* and now yearn to see *Dio*.
ja: 岩の背後の影にて、i peccatoriはDanteに留まるよう懇願し、自分たちの中に便りを届けられるalcunがいないか、心当たりがないかと問いかけた。彼らは、Dioの光によって悔い改めた罪人であり、今はDioに会うことを切望しているのだと明かした。

## Scene 58-63: Dante's Vow
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Dante* informs the *spiriti ben nati* that although he recognizes none of them, he will do whatever he can to help them for the sake of the peace he seeks while following *Virgilio*.
ja: 岩の背後の陰で、Danteはspiriti ben natiに対し、彼らの誰一人として心当たりはないが、Virgilioに従いながら追い求める平安のために、彼らを助けるべくできる限りのことをすると告げた。

## Scene 64-72: The First Soul's Request
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *uno* notes that *ciascun* trusts in *Dante* and requests that *Dante* offer prayers for him in a land near that of *Carlo*.
ja: 岩の背後の陰で、unoはciascunがDanteに信頼を寄せていることを述べ、Carloの地にほど近い場所で自らのために祈りを捧げるようDanteに請うた。

## Scene 73-84: The Death in the Marsh
en: At `*a l'ombra dietro al sasso*`, *Pier della Vigna* describes being wounded in the domain of `*li Antenori*` through the hatred of `*quel da Esti*`. He recounts how, while fleeing past `*Orïaco*`, he became entangled in a marsh and bled to death.
ja: 岩の背後の陰で、Pier della Vignaは、quel da Estiの憎悪によってli Antenoriの領地で傷を負わされたことを語る。彼はOrïacoを通り過ぎて逃走する途上、沼地に囚われて失血死に至った経緯を回想した。

## Scene 85-90: The Second Soul's Appearance
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Bonconte* asks *Dante* to aid his cause if he succeeds in his journey, mourning that he walks among the *anime* with a lowered brow because *Giovanna* and others no longer care for him.
ja: l'ombra dietro al sassoにて、Bonconteは、Giovannaらから忘れ去られてうつむいたままanimeの間を歩む己の境遇を嘆きながら、Danteに旅を成し遂げた暁には自らの願いを叶えてほしいと請い願った。

## Scene 91-93: Dante's Inquiry
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Dante* asks *Bonconte di Montefeltro* what force or twist of fate led him so far from his final battle that the location of his burial remained unknown.
ja: l'ombra dietro al sasso において、Dante は Bonconte di Montefeltro に、いかなる力、あるいは運命のいたずらが彼を最期の戦いから遠く引き離し、その埋葬地を誰にも知られぬままにしたのかと問いかけた。

## Scene 94-108: The Battle of Angels
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Bonconte di Montefeltro* tells *Dante* how he died in the name of *Maria* and asks that this account be shared with the *vivi*. He describes how *l'angel di Dio* claimed him while the *demonio* protested, complaining that a single tear had deprived him of the soul.
ja: 岩の背後の陰にて、Bonconte di MontefeltroはMariaの名においていかに死んだかをDanteに語り、この話をviviに伝えるよう願う。彼は、l'angel di Dioが自らを連れ去った際、たった一粒の涙によって魂を奪われたとdemonioが抗議した様子を詳述した。

## Scene 109-114: The Formation of Mist
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Dante* is told how a malicious will stirred up wind and smoke, mirroring the way humid vapor condenses in the cold.
ja: l'ombra dietro al sassoにて、Danteは、冷気の中で湿った蒸気が凝縮するように、ある悪意ある意志が風と煙を巻き起こしたという話を聞かされる。

## Scene 115-129: The River's Embrace
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Bonconte di Montefeltro* describes how torrential rains and rushing waters carried his frozen corpse away, stripping him of a cross he had made in his grief before burying him in the riverbed.
ja: 岩陰にて、Bonconte di Montefeltroは、豪雨と激流がいかにして凍てついた己の亡骸を押し流し、悲しみのなかで設えた十字架を奪い去ったうえに、彼を川底へと埋めたかを語る。

## Scene 130-136: The Plea of La Pia
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *la Pia* follows *Bonconte di Montefeltro* to ask *Dante* to remember her upon his return to the living, mentioning the *marito della Pia* who had once adorned her with a ring.
ja: 岩の背後の影で、la PiaはBonconte di Montefeltroに続き、かつて自分に指輪を贈ったmarito della Piaについて語りながら、現世へ戻るDanteに、どうか自分のことを思い出してほしいと請うた。
