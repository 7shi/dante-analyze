# Canto 08 — The Arrival of the Angels and the Encounter with the Malaspina

## Scene 1-18: The Evening Hymn
en: At the *fianco de la lacca*, while the sunset evokes the longing of *navicanti* for their *dolci amici* and *Dante* for his love, *una de l'alme* approaches *Dante* and sings a hymn to *Dio* with such sweetness that he is entranced. *L'altre* then join her in the hymn, all gazing upward together.
ja: 夕日がnavicantiにdolci amiciへの思慕を、Danteに最愛の人への切なさを呼び起こす湖畔で、una de l'almeがDanteに近づき、Dioへの賛美歌をあまりに甘美に歌ったため、彼はすっかり心を奪われてしまった。やがてl'altreもその賛美歌に加わり、皆揃って天を仰いだ。

## Scene 19-30: The Descent of the Angels
en: *Dante* urges the *lettor* to look closely at the truth while *quell'essercito gentile* waits in humble silence at the *fianco de la lacca*. Then, *due angeli* dressed in green descend from above, carrying blunt swords.
ja: Danteはlettorに真実を凝視するよう促す。quell'essercito gentileが湖のほとりで慎ましく静かに待つ中、緑の衣を纏ったdue angeliが、鈍い剣を携えて天から舞い降りた。

## Scene 31-36: The Crowd's Reaction
en: At the *fianco de la lacca*, *l'un* hovers above *Dante*, *Virgilio*, and *Sordello*, while *L'altro* descends to the opposite bank, confining the *gente* between them. *Dante* discerns the blond hair of the *angeli*, but the radiance of their faces overwhelms his vision.
ja: 湖の岸辺で、l'unはDante、Virgilio、Sordelloの上空に留まり、L'altroは対岸へと降りて、genteをその間に閉じ込めた。Danteはangeliの金髪を認めたが、その顔から放たれる眩い光に視界を奪われた。

## Scene 37-45: Sordello's Warning
en: *Sordello* explains that the *angeli* come from *Maria* to guard the *fianco de la lacca* against a coming threat, causing a terrified *Dante* to cling to *Virgilio*. *Sordello* then suggests they descend to converse with *le grandi ombre*.
ja: Sordelloは、迫りくる脅威から湖のほとりを守るためにMariaからangeliが遣わされたのだと説き、恐怖に駆られたDanteはVirgilioにしがみついた。そしてSordelloは、le grandi ombreと語らうために下方へ降りようと提案した。

## Scene 46-55: Meeting the Gentle Judge
en: At the *fianco de la lacca*, *Dante* and *Nin* recognize each other and approach for a warm greeting, with *Dante* expressing his joy that *Nin* is not among the *rei*.
ja: laccaのほとりで、DanteとNinは互いに気づいて親しげに近づき、DanteはNinがreiの一員ではないことに喜びを露わにした。

## Scene 56-63: The Inquiry of Origins
en: At the *fianco de la lacca*, *Nin* inquires when *Dante* arrived, and *Dante* reveals he reached the shore this morning while still in his first life. This revelation causes *Sordello* and *le grandi ombre* to recoil in sudden bewilderment.
ja: fianco de la laccaにて、Ninがいつ到着したのかを問うと、Danteはまだ第一の生にありながら今朝この岸に辿り着いたのだと明かした。その言葉に、Sordelloとle grandi ombreは不意に当惑し、後ずさりした。

## Scene 64-81: The Message for Giovanna
en: At the *fianco de la lacca*, *Sordello* turns to *Virgilio* while *Nin* urges *Currado* to witness the grace of *Dio*, then asks *Dante* to tell *Giovanna* to pray for him among *li 'nnocenti*. *Nin* reflects on the fleeting love of *Femmina* and doubts that *la sua madre* still loves him, wishing *il gallo di Gallura* had provided her burial.
ja: そのほとりにて、*Sordello*が*Virgilio*の方を向く一方で、*Nin*は*Currado*に*Dio*の慈悲を目の当たりにするよう促し、さらに*Dante*には*li 'nnocenti*の中で自分に祈るよう*Giovanna*に伝えてほしいと請うた。*Nin*は*Femmina*の儚い愛に思いを馳せ、*la sua madre*が今もなお自分を愛しているのかという疑念を抱きながら、*il gallo di Gallura*が彼女の埋葬を執り行っていたならと願うのであった。

## Scene 82-93: Celestial Observations
en: At the *fianco de la lacca*, *Dante* gazes intently at three glowing lights in the heavens, prompting *Virgilio* to explain the movement of the stars and how they have shifted since the morning.
ja: fianco de la laccaにて、Danteが天に輝く三つの光をじっと見つめると、Virgilioは星々の運行と、朝からそれらがどのように位置を変えたのかについて説いた。

## Scene 94-108: The Serpent's Intrusion
en: While *Virgilio* speaks in *la picciola vallea*, *Sordello* draws *Dante* aside to point out *l nostro avversaro*, a serpent like the one that tempted *Eva*. As *l nostro avversaro* glides through the grass, *li angeli* frighten the creature away and fly back to their posts.
ja: Virgilioが小さな谷で語っている間、SordelloはDanteを脇へ引き寄せ、Evaを誘惑したあの蛇のようなl nostro avversaroを指し示した。l nostro avversaroが草むらを滑るように進むと、li angeliがその怪物を追い払い、再びそれぞれの持ち場へと舞い戻った。

## Scene 109-120: The Identification of Currado
en: In *la picciola vallea*, *Currado Malaspina*, who had gathered near the mentioned *Nin*, identifies himself as a descendant of a forebear and asks *Dante* for news of his homeland, describing *Virgilio* as a lamp guiding the way.
ja: la picciola valleaにて、前述のNinの近くにいたCurrado Malaspinaは、自らが先祖の末裔であることを名乗り、Virgilioを道を導く灯火に例えながら、Danteに故郷の知らせを求めた。

## Scene 121-132: Praise for the Malaspina
en: In *la picciola vallea*, *Dante* tells *Currado Malaspina* that although he has never visited their lands, the fame of the *Malaspina* is known even to *i segnori*. He declares that the *Malaspina* remain virtuous in generosity and courage, unlike a world twisted by *il capo reo*.
ja: その小さな谷にて、DanteはCurrado Malaspinaに、その地を訪れたことはないが、Malaspinaの名声はi segnoriにまで届いていると語った。そして、il capo reoによって歪められた世界とは異なり、Malaspinaは寛大さと勇気において高潔であり続けていると宣言した。

## Scene 133-139: The Judge's Dismissal
en: In *la picciola vallea*, *Dante* is urged to depart, warned that his courteous opinion of *Nin* and *Currado* will be nailed to his head if he lingers.
ja: 小さな谷にいたDanteは、もしこの場に留まればNinとCurradoへの礼節ある評価が頭に釘付けにされるだろうと警告され、立ち去るよう促された。
