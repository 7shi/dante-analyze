# Canto 04 — The Ascent of the Mountain and the Encounter with Belacqua

## Scene 1-12: Philosophical Prologue
en: At the *piè del monte*, it is reflected that when *l'anima* focuses its power, *l'uom* loses track of time, refuting the error that one *un'anima* could ignite another.
ja: 山の麓にて、l'animaがその力を凝らし、l'uomが時の流れを忘れるとき、一つのun'animaが他方に火を灯しうるという誤謬は覆される。

## Scene 13-18: The Voice in the Dark
en: At the *piè del monte*, *Dante* is so absorbed in listening to *Belacqua* that he fails to notice the sun rising fifty degrees. This distraction ends when he and *Virgilio* reach a place where *quell' anime* shout that they have arrived at their destination.
ja: 山のふもとで、DanteはBelacquaの話にすっかり聞き入っていたため、太陽が五十度も昇ったことに気づかなかった。彼とVirgilioがある場所に辿り着き、quell' animeが目的地に到着したと叫んだことで、ようやくその心酔は解かれた。

## Scene 19-24: The Simile of the Vineyard
en: Drawing a comparison to the way `*l'uom de la villa*` tends grapes, `*Virgilio*` and `*Dante*` proceed alone along `*la calla*` as the `*schiera*` parts from them.
ja: `l'uom de la villa`が葡萄の手入れをするかのごとく、`schiera`が分かれて道を開けるなか、`Virgilio`と`Dante`はただ二人でその道を歩み進んでいった。

## Scene 25-30: The Flight of Desire
en: Reflecting on his earthly travels, *Dante* realizes that on *la calla* he must fly with the wings of great desire, following *Virgilio*, who offers him hope and light.
ja: 地上での旅路を振り返り、Danteは、希望と光を授けるVirgilioに従い、カラーの花の上を大いなる切望の翼で飛ぶべきなのだと悟った。

## Scene 31-35: The Difficult Climb
en: Scrambling through the *sasso rotto* with hands and feet as the narrow cliffs press in, *Dante* and *Virgilio* eventually reach the summit's open slope.
ja: 狭い崖に圧迫されながら、手足を使って砕けた岩場を這い上がった Dante と Virgilio は、ついに山頂の開けた斜面へと辿り着いた。

## Scene 36-39: Virgil's Command
en: In the *sasso rotto*, *Dante* asks *Virgilio* which way they should go, and *Virgilio* instructs him to simply follow behind until *alcuna scorta saggia* appears.
ja: 砕けた岩場にて、Danteがどちらへ進むべきかを尋ねると、Virgilioはalcuna scorta saggiaが現れるまで、ただ後ろについてくるようにと指示した。

## Scene 40-48: The Exhausted Climber
en: In the *sasso rotto*, *Dante* falters under the steepness of the ascent and calls to *Virgilio*, fearing he has been left behind in his exhaustion. *Virgilio* encourages him to press on toward a ledge slightly further up the slope.
ja: sasso rottoの険しい登りにDanteはたじろぎ、疲労のあまり取り残されたのではないかと恐れてVirgilioを呼ぶ。Virgilioは、斜面の少し先にある岩棚を目指して突き進むよう彼を励ました。

## Scene 49-54: The Rest on the Summit
en: Spurred by the words of *Virgilio*, *Dante* catches up to him in the *sasso rotto* at *l'orlo suppremo de l'alta ripa*. Together, they sit and look back toward the east, finding comfort in the view of their ascent.
ja: Virgilioの言葉に促され、Danteは高い崖の最上端にある砕けた岩場で彼に追いついた。二人は共に腰を下ろして東の方を振り返り、自らが登ってきた道の景色に安らぎを見出した。

## Scene 55-60: Observing the Sun
en: At *l'orlo suppremo de l'alta ripa* in the *sasso rotto*, *Dante* gazes at the sun and is struck by the light hitting them from the left. *Virgilio* observes that *Dante* stands completely mesmerized by the radiance.
ja: 切り立った崖の最上端、砕けた岩場にて、Danteは太陽を凝視し、左から二人を照らす光に打たれた。その眩い輝きに完全に心を奪われて立ち尽くすDanteの姿を、Virgilioは見守っていた。

## Scene 61-75: The Astronomical Lecture
en: At *l'orlo suppremo de l'alta ripa* in the *sasso rotto*, *Virgilio* explains the solar trajectory to *Dante*, mentioning *Castore* and *Poluce* and contrasting the sun's orderly path with that of *Fetòn*.
ja: 切り立った崖の最上端、砕かれた岩場において、VirgilioはCastoreとPoluceの名を挙げながらDanteに太陽の軌道を説き、その秩序ある運行をFetònのそれと対比させた。

## Scene 76-84: Dante's Astronomical Observation
en: At `*l'orlo suppremo de l'alta ripa*` in the `*sasso rotto*`, `*Dante*` tells `*Virgilio*` that he now clearly perceives the middle circle of the celestial motion, citing the perspective of the mentioned `*li Ebrei*`.
ja: `sasso rotto`にある`l'orlo suppremo de l'alta ripa`において、`Dante`は前述の`li Ebrei`の視点を引き合いに出しながら、天球運動の中央の円を今や明確に捉えていることを`Virgilio`に語った。

## Scene 85-87: A Request for Guidance
en: At *l'orlo suppremo de l'alta ripa* in the *sasso rotto*, *Dante* asks *Virgilio* how much further they must go, for the slope rises higher than his eyes can see.
ja: 砕け散った岩に囲まれた高い岸壁の最上端で、DanteはVirgilioに、あとどれほど先まで行かねばならないのかと問う。目の前の斜面は、視界の及ばぬほど高く切り立っていた。

## Scene 88-96: The Nature of the Mountain
en: At *l'orlo suppremo de l'alta ripa* in the *sasso rotto*, *Virgilio* explains to *Dante* that the mountain becomes easier the higher *om* ascends. He tells *Dante* that once the climb feels effortless, he will have reached the end of the path.
ja: l'orlo suppremo de l'alta ripa in the sasso rottoにて、VirgilioはDanteに、omが登れば登るほど山道は容易になると説いた。そして、登ることに苦労を感じなくなったとき、道の終わりに辿り着いたのだとDanteに告げた。

## Scene 97-102: A Voice from the Shadows
en: As soon as `*Virgilio*` finishes speaking at `*l'orlo suppremo de l'alta ripa*` in the `*sasso rotto*`, `*Belacqua*` calls out to `*Dante*` about his sitting, leading `*Dante*` and `*Virgilio*` to discover a large boulder they had previously overlooked.
ja: `sasso rotto` の `l'orlo suppremo de l'alta ripa` にて `Virgilio` が語り終えた途端、`Belacqua` が座っている `Dante` に声をかけたことで、`Dante` と `Virgilio` はそれまで見落としていた大きな岩に気づくこととなった。

## Scene 103-108: The Slumbering Figure
en: *Dante* and *Virgilio* move *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, where they encounter *persone* lounging as *l'uom* might do out of laziness. Among them is a weary *Belacqua*, sitting with his face tucked between his knees.
ja: DanteとVirgilioが岩の背後の陰へ進むと、そこにはl'uomが怠惰ゆえにそうするように、だらしなくくつろぐpersoneたちがいた。その中には、疲れ切って顔を膝の間にうずめて座り込むBelacquaの姿があった。

## Scene 109-114: The Recognition of Belacqua
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Dante* draws *Virgilio*'s attention to the profoundly negligent *Belacqua*, who looks up only to dismissively tell *Dante* to press on.
ja: l'ombra dietro al sasso において、Dante は Virgilio に、ひどく怠慢な Belacqua の存在を気づかせた。Belacqua は顔を上げたが、Dante に先へ進めと突き放すように告げるだけだった。

## Scene 115-122: The Encounter with Belacqua
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Dante* recognizes *Belacqua* and approaches him despite his own lingering anxiety. *Belacqua* barely raises his head to ask a curt question about the sun, and *Dante* is moved to a slight smile by his profound indolence.
ja: l'ombra dietro al sassoにて、DanteはBelacquaに気づき、拭いきれぬ不安を抱えながらも彼へと歩み寄った。Belacquaはかろうじて頭を上げ、太陽についてぶっきらぼうに問いかけたが、その底知れぬ怠惰さに、Danteはふとわずかな微笑みを浮かべた。

## Scene 123-126: Questioning the Sloth
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Dante* tells *Belacqua* that he no longer feels pity for him and asks whether he sits curled up awaiting an escort or has simply reverted to his habitual indolence.
ja: l'ombra dietro al sassoにて、DanteはBelacquaに、もはや貴様に憐れみなど感じないと言い放った。そして、案内人を待って丸まっているのか、あるいは単にいつもの怠惰に身を任せているだけなのかと問いかけた。

## Scene 127-135: Belacqua's Excuse
en: At `*a l'ombra dietro al sasso*`, *Belacqua* asks *Dante* why he should ascend, explaining that `*l'angel di Dio*` will not let him pass until he has waited for a duration equal to the time he spent procrastinating in his earthly life.
ja: `a l'ombra dietro al sasso`にて、BelacquaはDanteに何故登るのかと問い、地上で物事を先延ばしにした時間に等しい期間を待たぬ限り、l'angel di Dioは自分を通さないのだと説明した。

## Scene 136-139: Resuming the Journey
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Virgilio* climbs ahead and urges *Dante* to hurry, noting that it is now midday and night has already reached *Morrocco*.
ja: 岩の背後の陰で、Virgilioは先を登り、今は正午でありMorroccoにはすでに夜が訪れていると告げて、Danteに急ぐよう促した。
