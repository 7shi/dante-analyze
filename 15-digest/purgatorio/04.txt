# Canto 04 — The Ascent of the Mountain and the Encounter with Belacqua

## Scene 1-12: Philosophical Prologue
en: At the `*piè del monte*`, there is a meditation on how `*l'uom*` loses all sense of time when `*l'anima*` is absorbed in pleasure or pain. This refutes the error that `*un'anima*` could exist separately from another within `*l'uom*`.
ja: `piè del monte`において、`l'anima`が快楽や苦痛に没頭するとき、`l'uom`がいかに時の感覚をすべて失うかという思索が綴られている。これは、`l'uom`の中に`un'anima`が他から切り離されて存在しうるという誤謬を退けるものである。

## Scene 13-18: The Voice in the Dark
en: At the *piè del monte*, *Dante* is so absorbed in observing *Belacqua* that he loses track of time until he and *Virgilio* are hailed by *quell' anime*, who announce they have reached their destination.
ja: 山の麓にて、DanteはBelacquaを観察することに没頭し、時の経過を忘れていた。そこにquell' animeが彼とVirgilioに声をかけ、目的地に到着したことを告げた。

## Scene 19-24: The Simile of the Vineyard
en: *Dante* evokes the image of how *l'uom de la villa* prunes grapes as he and *Virgilio* proceed along *la calla*, now that the *schiera* has parted from them.
ja: schieraが彼らのもとを去り、DanteとVirgilioが路地を進むなか、Danteはl'uom de la villaが葡萄を剪定するさまに思いを馳せた。

## Scene 25-30: The Flight of Desire
en: Contrasting his former earthly journeys on foot, *Dante* now ascends *la calla* propelled by the wings of great desire, following *Virgilio*, who offers him hope and light.
ja: かつての地上での歩みとは対照的に、Dante は今、強き切望という翼に運ばれ、希望と光を授ける Virgilio に続いてカラーの道を昇りゆく。

## Scene 31-35: The Difficult Climb
en: *Dante* and *Virgilio* scramble through the *sasso rotto*, using both hands and feet to navigate the narrow cliffs until they reach the upper edge and emerge onto an open slope.
ja: DanteとVirgilioは、手足をついて狭い崖をよじ登り、砕けた岩場を通り抜けて上端に達すると、開けた斜面へと出た。

## Scene 36-39: Virgil's Command
en: At the *sasso rotto* on *l'orlo suppremo de l'alta ripa*, *Dante* asks *Virgilio* which way they should go, but *Virgilio* tells him to simply follow him up the mountain until *alcuna scorta saggia* appears.
ja: 高い岸の最上端にある砕けた岩場にて、Danteはどちらへ進むべきかをVirgilioに尋ねたが、Virgilioはalcuna scorta saggiaが現れるまで、ただ自分に従って山を登るようにと告げた。

## Scene 40-48: The Exhausted Climber
en: Exhausted by the steep slope at the *sasso rotto* on *l'orlo suppremo de l'alta ripa*, *Dante* calls out to *Virgilio* for support. *Virgilio* encourages him to persevere until he reaches a ledge where the path turns.
ja: 高い岸壁の最上端、崩れた岩場の急斜面に疲れ果てた Dante は、支えを求めて Virgilio に声を上げた。Virgilio は、道が折れ曲がる棚に辿り着くまで諦めずに進むよう、彼を鼓舞した。

## Scene 49-54: The Rest on the Summit
en: Motivated by the words of *Virgilio*, *Dante* struggles upward until he catches up to him on the *sasso rotto*. There, *Dante* and *Virgilio* sit and face east, gazing back upon the path they have climbed.
ja: Virgilioの言葉に励まされ、Danteはsasso rottoで彼に追いつくまで、もがきながら上へと登った。そこでDanteとVirgilioは東を向き、自分たちが登ってきた道を振り返りながら、腰を下ろした。

## Scene 55-60: Observing the Sun
en: At the *sasso rotto*, *Dante* looks first to the low shores and then to the sun, noting that the light strikes them from the left. *Virgilio* observes that *Dante* is completely mesmerized by the radiance of the light.
ja: 砕かれた岩のところで、Danteはまず低い海岸に、そして太陽に目を向け、光が左側から差し込んでいることに気づく。Virgilioは、Danteがその光の輝きにすっかり心を奪われている様子を見守っていた。

## Scene 61-75: The Astronomical Lecture
en: At the *sasso rotto*, *Virgilio* explains the celestial movements to *Dante*, referencing *Castore* and *Poluce* to illustrate the Zodiac's rotation. To clarify the sun's trajectory, *Virgilio* contrasts the orderly heavens with the chaotic path of *Fetòn*.
ja: sasso rottoにおいて、VirgilioはCastoreとPoluceを引き合いに黄道帯の回転を説き、Danteに天体の運行を詳述した。さらに、太陽の軌道を明確にするべく、秩序ある天界とFetònの混沌たる道筋を対比させて見せた。

## Scene 76-84: Dante's Astronomical Observation
en: At the *sasso rotto*, *Dante* tells *Virgilio* that he now perceives the movements of the heavens with a clarity he once lacked, explaining the position of the middle circle in relation to how it was observed by *li Ebrei*.
ja: 砕かれた岩のところで、DanteはVirgilioに、かつては欠けていた明晰さをもって今は天の運行を捉えていると語り、li Ebreiによる観測に照らして中圏の位置を説明した。

## Scene 85-87: A Request for Guidance
en: At the *sasso rotto*, *Dante* asks *Virgilio* how much further they have to travel, noting that the mountain rises higher than his eyes can see.
ja: sasso rottoにて、Danteは山が目の届かぬほど高くそびえ立っていることに気づき、あとどれほど進まねばならぬのかをVirgilioに問いかけた。

## Scene 88-96: The Nature of the Mountain
en: At the *sasso rotto* on *l'orlo suppremo de l'alta ripa*, *Virgilio* explains to *Dante* that the mountain becomes easier to climb as the *om* ascends, and that *Dante* will reach the end of the path when the effort feels as light as sailing downstream.
ja: 高い岸の最上端にある砕かれた岩にて、VirgilioはDanteに、omが登れば登るほど山は登りやすくなり、その労苦が川を下る帆走のように軽やかに感じられたとき、Danteは道の終わりに辿り着くのだと説いた。

## Scene 97-102: A Voice from the Shadows
en: As soon as *Virgilio* finishes speaking, *Belacqua* calls out from nearby, questioning if *Dante* has finally finished sitting. At the sound of the voice, *Dante* and *Virgilio* turn to discover a great boulder at the *sasso rotto* that they had not previously noticed.
ja: Virgilioが話し終えるとすぐに、近くからBelacquaが、Danteはようやく座り終えたのかと声をかけてきた。その声にDanteとVirgilioが振り返ると、そこにはそれまで気づかなかった、砕けた岩場の巨岩があった。

## Scene 103-108: The Slumbering Figure
en: *Dante* and *Virgilio* move *a l'ombra dietro al sasso* and find *persone* resting as the mentioned *l'uom* would out of laziness. Among them, *Belacqua* sits huddled with his face tucked low between his knees.
ja: DanteとVirgilioが岩の背後の陰へと進むと、そこには先ほどのl'uomのように怠惰に身を休めるpersoneたちがいた。その中に、Belacquaが膝の間に顔を深く埋め、身を縮めて座り込んでいた。

## Scene 109-114: The Recognition of Belacqua
en: *A l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Dante* asks *Virgilio* to observe the extreme indolence of *Belacqua*. *Belacqua* looks up and dismissively tells *Dante* to continue his ascent, since he is so capable.
ja: 岩の陰で、DanteはBelacquaの極端な怠惰ぶりをVirgilioに見てほしいと求めた。顔を上げたBelacquaは、それほど有能なのだからそのまま登り続けろと、Danteを突き放すように言い放った。

## Scene 115-122: The Encounter with Belacqua
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Dante* recognizes *Belacqua* and approaches him despite a lingering anxiety. *Belacqua* barely raises his head to ask if *Dante* has noted the sun's position, an act of indolence that brings a slight smile to *Dante*'s lips.
ja: 岩の背後の陰で、DanteはBelacquaを認め、拭いきれぬ不安を抱えながらも彼へと近づいた。Belacquaはかろうじて頭を上げ、太陽の位置に気づいたかを問いかけたが、その怠惰な振る舞いにDanteの唇にはかすかな微笑みが浮かんだ。

## Scene 123-126: Questioning the Sloth
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Dante* tells *Belacqua* he no longer feels pity for him and asks why he remains huddled, wondering if he awaits an escort or has simply returned to his habitual indolence.
ja: 岩の背後の陰で、DanteはBelacquaにもはや憐れみなど感じないと告げ、なぜ縮こまったままでいるのか、案内人を待っているのか、あるいは単にいつもの怠惰に戻っただけなのかと問いかけた。

## Scene 127-135: Belacqua's Excuse
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Belacqua* tells *Dante* that *l'angel di Dio* bars him from the ascent until the heavens have turned for a time equal to the life he wasted in indolence, noting that prayers not offered in grace are unheard in heaven.
ja: 岩の裏の陰にて、BelacquaはDanteに、怠惰に費やした人生に等しい時間が天を巡るまでl'angel di Dioが昇天を禁じていると告げ、恩寵なき祈りは天に届かぬものであると付け加えた。

## Scene 136-139: Resuming the Journey
en: At *a l'ombra dietro al sasso*, *Virgilio* climbs ahead and urges *Dante* to hurry, noting that it is already midday while night covers *Morrocco*.
ja: 岩の背後の陰で、Virgilioは先に登り、Morroccoが夜に包まれている一方でこちらはすでに正午であると告げて、Danteに急ぐよう促した。
