# Canto 26 — Encounters with the Souls of the Lustful

## Scene 1-9: Sunset in the Inferno
en: While *Dante*, *Virgilio*, and *Stazio* walk along *l'orlo*, *Virgilio* urges *Dante* to stay alert. As the sun sets, *Dante*'s shadow makes the flames appear more intense, drawing the attention of *molt' ombre*.
ja: Dante, Virgilio, Stazioが縁に沿って歩く中、VirgilioはDanteに警戒を怠らぬよう促した。日が沈むにつれ、Danteの影が炎をいっそう激しく際立たせ、それがmolt' ombreたちの注意を引いた。

## Scene 10-15: The Shadows Observe Dante
en: The *lussuriosi* on *l'orlo* begin to discuss *Dante*, observing that he does not seem a fictitious body, and they edge closer to him while remaining within the flames.
ja: 縁にいるlussuriosiたちがDanteについて語り合い、彼が実体のない幻影ではないことに気づくと、炎の中に留まったままじりじりと彼に近づいてきた。

## Scene 16-24: An Inquiry from the Flames
en: A *lussurioso* on *l'orlo* addresses *Dante*, asking on behalf of all the *lussuriosi* why he still casts a shadow as if he had not yet entered the net of death.
ja: 縁に立つ一人のlussuriosoがDanteに語りかけ、すべてのlussuriosiを代表して、死の網に囚われていないかのようにしてなぜ今も影を落としているのかと問いかけた。

## Scene 25-30: Arrival of the New Group
en: As one of the *lussuriosi* speaks to *Dante* on *l'orlo*, *Dante* is distracted from responding by the appearance of *gente* who approach, leaving him captivated.
ja: 縁で一人のlussuriosiがDanteに語りかけていたが、近づいてくるgenteの姿に気を取られたDanteは、答えようとしていたことさえ忘れ、すっかり心を奪われてしまった。

## Scene 31-36: The Swarming Shadows
en: On *l'orlo*, *Dante* watches *l'ombra* rushing to exchange brief kisses, swarming like ants as they spy on the path and their fortunes.
ja: 縁に立つDanteは、道行きと自らの運命を窺いながら、短い口づけを交わそうと蟻のように群がり急ぐl'ombraの姿を眺めていた。

## Scene 37-42: The Divided Proclamations
en: As soon as the *lussuriosi* on *l'orlo* finish their friendly welcomes, *la nova gente* begins to shout, with some speaking of *Pasife*.
ja: 縁にいるlussuriosiたちが友好的な歓迎を終えるやいなや、la nova genteが叫び出し、中にはPasifeについて語る者もいた。

## Scene 43-48: The Parting of the Souls
en: On *l'orlo*, the *lussuriosi* split into two groups, one departing and another arriving. Weeping, they return to their original chants and cries.
ja: その縁にて、lussuriosiは去りゆく者と辿り着く者の二つの群れに分かれた。彼らは涙に暮れながら、元の詠唱と叫びへと戻っていく。

## Scene 49-51: The Return of the First Group
en: On *l'orlo*, the *lussuriosi* who had previously questioned *Dante* draw near him again, their expressions attentive and eager to hear his response.
ja: その縁にて、かつてDanteに問いかけたlussuriosiたちが再び彼に歩み寄り、熱心な面持ちでその答えを待ちわびていた。

## Scene 52-66: Dante's Explanation
en: On *l'orlo*, *Dante* explains to the *anime* that he is physically present through the grace of *Beatrice*, who is mentioned as his intercessor, and asks the *anime* to reveal their identities and those of the other *anime* who just departed.
ja: その縁にて、Danteは、とりなし手であるBeatriceの恩寵により自らが肉体を持ってここに在るのだとanimeに語り、彼らと、先ほど去っていった他のanimeたちが何者であるかを明かすよう求めた。

## Scene 67-72: The Shadows' Confusion
en: On *l'orlo*, *ciascun' ombra* is momentarily stunned by the physical nature of *Dante* and the reaction of *Virgilio*, before eventually shaking off their stupor.
ja: その縁にて、ciascun' ombraはDanteの身体性とVirgilioの反応に一瞬呆然とするが、やがてはその茫然とした心地から立ち直った。

## Scene 73-87: Guido's Identification
en: On *l'orlo*, *Guido da Montefeltro* tells *Dante* that he is blessed to learn from this experience, contrasting the departing *gente*—whose sodomitic lust is illustrated by the mentioned *Cesar* and *Regina*—with *the first group of souls*, who similarly followed beastly appetites.
ja: l'orloにて、Guido da MontefeltroはDanteに、この経験から学びを得られることは祝福であると語り、CesarやReginaに象徴される同性愛的な情欲に耽って去りゆくgenteと、同じく獣のごとき欲望に従ったthe first group of soulsとを対比させた。

## Scene 88-93: The Pride of the Poet
en: On *l'orlo*, *Guido Guinizzelli* explains to *Dante* that although the *anime* are not yet permitted to disclose their identities, he will disregard this restriction and reveal his name while he continues to purge his sins.
ja: その縁にて、Guido GuinizzelliはDanteに対し、animeたちが正体を明かすことはまだ許されていないものの、自らはこの制限を無視して、罪を浄化し続ける間もその名を明かすと語った。

## Scene 94-102: Dante's Emotional Response
en: On *l'orlo*, *Dante* is overcome with sadness upon hearing the name of *Guido Guinizzelli* and *li altri miei miglior*, comparing his grief to that of the *due figli* of *Ligurgo* upon seeing *la madre*. He walks away pensively, gazing at *Guido Guinizzelli* while keeping a distance from the flames.
ja: 縁に立ったDanteは、Guido Guinizzelliとli altri miei migliorの名を聞いて深い悲しみに襲われ、その嘆きをla madreを見たdue figli of Ligurgoのそれに例えた。彼は炎から距離を置きながら、Guido Guinizzelliをじっと見つめ、物思いにふけって歩き去った。

## Scene 103-105: Dante's Readiness to Serve
en: On *l'orlo*, after having satisfied his gaze, *Dante* offers his service with a firm affirmation to make his sincerity believable.
ja: l'orloにて、十分に辺りを眺め終えたDanteは、自らの誠実さを信じさせるため、断固たる口調で仕えることを申し出た。

## Scene 106-111: Guido's Request
en: On *l'orlo*, *Guido Guinizzelli* tells *Dante* that his words have left an indelible impression and asks him to explain the reason for the affection he displays in his speech and gaze.
ja: l'orloの縁で、Guido GuinizzelliはDanteに、彼の言葉が消えぬ印象を心に刻んだと語り、その言葉遣いと眼差しに滲む慈しみの理由を説明するよう求めた。

## Scene 112-114: Dante's Compliment
en: On *l'orlo*, *Dante* tells *Guido Guinizzelli* that his sweet words will remain beloved for as long as the modern style persists.
ja: その縁で、DanteはGuido Guinizzelliに、新しい様式が存続する限り、彼の甘美な言葉は愛され続けるだろうと語った。

## Scene 115-126: Praise for Arnaut Daniel
en: On *l'orlo*, *Guido Guinizzelli* points out *Arnaut Daniel* to *Dante*, praising his poetic mastery while noting that *li stolti* often mistake popular acclaim for truth. He compares this to how *molti antichi di Guittone* once lauded *Guittone d'Arezzo* until the truth was eventually recognized by *più persone*.
ja: その縁にて、Guido GuinizzelliはDanteにArnaut Danielを紹介し、その卓越した詩才を称えながらも、li stoltiはしばしば大衆の喝采を真実と見誤るものであると説いた。彼はこれを、かつてmolti antichi di GuittoneがGuittone d'Arezzoを称賛し、やがてpiù personeによって真実が認められたことになぞらえて語った。

## Scene 127-132: A Prayer for the Departed
en: On *l'orlo*, *Guido Guinizzelli* asks *Dante* to use his privilege as a living man to pray a *Pater Noster* in the cloister of *Cristo* for the benefit of the *lussuriosi*, who can no longer act or sin in the world.
ja: その縁にて、Guido GuinizzelliはDanteに、もはや現世で行動することも罪を犯すことも叶わぬlussuriosiのために、生者としての特権を用いてCristoの回廊で主の祈りを捧げてほしいと請うた。

## Scene 133-135: The Disappearance of Guido
en: On *l'orlo*, *Guido Guinizzelli* disappears into the flames like a fish sinking into water, perhaps to make room for the other *lussuriosi* who were waiting nearby.
ja: 縁にて、Guido Guinizzelliは水に沈みゆく魚のように炎の中へと消えていった。おそらくは、近くで待っていた他のlussuriosiに場所を空けるためであったろう。

## Scene 136-141: The Arrival of Arnaut Daniel
en: On *l'orlo*, *Dante* approaches *Arnaut Daniel* and expresses his deep admiration for him. *Arnaut Daniel* responds that he is delighted by such courtesy and has no wish to hide himself from the living poet.
ja: l'orloにて、DanteはArnaut Danielに歩み寄り、彼への深い敬愛を伝えた。Arnaut Danielは、そのような礼遇に喜び、生ける詩人の前から自らを隠そうとは思わないと応えた。

## Scene 142-148: Arnaut's Lament and Departure
en: On *l'orlo*, *Arnaut Daniel* describes his state of weeping for past folly and hoping for future joy, then asks *Dante* to remember his pain before disappearing back into the refining fire.
ja: 縁にて、Arnaut Danielは過去の愚行に涙し、未来の喜びを願う自らのありようを語った。そしてDanteに自らの苦しみを記憶にとどめるよう請うと、再び浄化の炎の中へと消え去っていった。
