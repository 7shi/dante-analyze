# Canto 31 — The Sphinx's Instruction and the Celestial Dance

## Scene 1-6: The Sphinx's Accusation
en: At the *coscia del carro*, *Beatrice* addresses *Dante* with a biting tone, insisting that he provide a confession to match the accusation she has leveled against him.
ja: coscia del carroにて、Beatriceは辛辣な口調でDanteに語りかけ、自らが突きつけた罪状にふさわしい告白をするよう彼に迫った。

## Scene 7-15: Dante's Hesitant Response
en: At the *coscia del carro*, *Dante* is so overcome by confusion and fear that his voice fails him, prompting *Beatrice* to demand an answer. He manages only a faint "yes," which is so quiet that *Beatrice* must read his lips to understand it.
ja: coscia del carroにて、Danteは混乱と恐怖に飲み込まれて声を失い、Beatriceは彼に答えを促した。彼はかろうじてかすかな「はい」と答えたが、その声があまりに小さかったため、Beatriceは彼の唇の動きを読み取らなければならなかった。

## Scene 16-21: The Burst of Sorrow
en: At the *coscia del carro*, *Dante* snaps under the weight of a heavy burden, erupting in tears and sighs as his voice fails him.
ja: coscia del carroにて、Danteは重すぎる負荷に耐えかねて崩れ落ち、言葉を失ったまま、溢れ出す涙と溜息に身を任せた。

## Scene 22-30: The Sphinx's Inquiry
en: At the *coscia del carro*, *Beatrice* asks *Dante* what obstacles or chains diverted him from his desire for the Good and what advantages *li altri* possessed that allowed them to progress ahead of him.
ja: 車の傍らで、BeatriceはDanteに問いかけた。いかなる障害や鎖が彼を善への希求から逸らしたのか、そしてli altriはどのような利点を持っていたがゆえに、彼を追い越して先に進むことができたのか。

## Scene 31-36: The Confession of Sin
en: At the *coscia del carro*, *Dante* struggles to regain his voice and, weeping, confesses that false earthly pleasures diverted his path after a certain face disappeared from his sight.
ja: 車の傍らで、Danteはかろうじて声を絞り出し、ある人の顔が視界から消えた後、地上の偽りの快楽に惑わされて道を踏み外したことを、涙ながらに告白した。

## Scene 37-48: The Command to Listen
en: At the *coscia del carro*, *Beatrice* tells *Dante* that although *Dio* already knows his guilt, his contrition mitigates his judgment. She commands *Dante* to stop weeping and listen to how he must now proceed.
ja: 御車の腿の部分で、BeatriceはDanteに、Dioはすでに彼の罪をご存じだが、悔い改めたことでその裁きは軽減されるのだと告げた。そして、涙を止めてこれからいかに進むべきかを聞くように、と彼に命じた。

## Scene 49-63: The Vanity of Earthly Beauty
en: At the *coscia del carro*, *Beatrice* argues that since her own earthly beauty was the highest possible pleasure, her death should have served as a warning for *Dante* to reject all other fleeting mortal desires. She chides him for continuing to chase lesser temptations instead of lifting his soul to follow her.
ja: 車の腿の部分で、Beatriceは、自らの地上の美こそが至上の快楽であった以上、その死はDanteが他のあらゆる儚い現世の欲望を捨てるための警告となるべきだったと説く。魂を高めて彼女に従うのではなく、より劣った誘惑を追い求め続けた彼を、彼女は咎める。

## Scene 64-69: A Call to Courage
en: At the *coscia del carro*, *Dante* stands silent and repentant like shamed *fanciulli*, until *Beatrice* commands him to look up to hear her, warning that gazing upon her will only increase his sorrow.
ja: 車の腿の傍らで、Danteは恥じらうfanciulliのように沈黙し、悔恨に伏していた。そこにBeatriceが、話を聞くために顔を上げるよう彼に命じるが、彼女を見つめればただ悲しみが深まるだけだと警告した。

## Scene 70-75: The Simile of the Stripped Tree
en: At the *coscia del carro*, *Dante* compares himself to a sturdy tree stripped bare by winds from the land of *Iarba*, mentioning the fragility of *men di resistenza*. When *Beatrice* compels him to look up, *Dante* recognizes the painful truth of her argument.
ja: 車の腿の部分で、Danteは自らをIarbaの地から吹く風に葉を剥ぎ取られた強靭な樹木に例え、men di resistenzaの脆さを語った。Beatriceに視線を上げるよう促されると、Danteは彼女の主張が突きつける痛切な真実を悟った。

## Scene 76-84: The Vision of Beatrice
en: At the *coscia del carro*, *Dante* lifts his gaze and, through the influence of *quelle prime creature*, sees *Beatrice* looking up at *Cristo*. He observes that *Beatrice* appears more radiant and superior than she had been before.
ja: 車の側面にいたDanteが視線を上げると、quelle prime creatureの導きによって、Cristoを仰ぎ見るBeatriceの姿が見えた。そのBeatriceは、以前よりもさらに光り輝き、いっそう気高く見えた。

## Scene 85-90: The Sting of Repentance
en: At the *coscia del carro*, the sting of repentance pierces *Dante*, making *Beatrice*'s love feel like an enemy to his guilt. Overwhelmed by this recognition, *Dante* falls defeated while *Beatrice* observes the cause she has provided.
ja: coscia del carroにて、悔恨の刺がDanteを貫き、Beatriceの愛は彼の罪悪感に対する敵であるかのように感じられた。その自覚に圧倒され、Danteが力なく崩れ落ちるのを、Beatriceは自らがもたらしたその原因として静かに見つめていた。

## Scene 91-96: Crossing the River
en: At the *coscia del carro*, *Dante* regains his strength and sees *Beatrice*, who commands him to fear her before drawing him into the river up to his throat. *Beatrice* then glides effortlessly across the water, towing *Dante* behind her.
ja: 車の腿のような場所で気力を取り戻したDanteは、Beatriceの姿を目にする。彼女は彼に自分を畏れるよう命じると、その身を喉まで川へと引きずり込み、そのまま軽やかに水面を滑りながらDanteを後ろに曳いてゆく。

## Scene 97-105: Arrival at the Blessed Shore
en: At the *beata riva*, *Beatrice* embraces *Dante* and immerses him in the water for purification. She then presents him to *le quattro belle*, who welcome him by surrounding him with their arms as they dance.
ja: 祝福された岸辺にて、BeatriceはDanteを抱きしめ、浄化のために彼を水に浸した。それから彼女が彼をle quattro belleに引き合わせると、彼女たちは踊りながら彼を腕で囲んで歓迎した。

## Scene 106-111: The Nymphs' Identity
en: At the *beata riva*, the *ninfe* explain that they are stars and former handmaidens to the mentioned *Beatrice*, noting that *le tre* will look more deeply into his inner light.
ja: beata rivaにて、ninfeたちは自らが星であり、かつてBeatriceに仕えた侍女であったことを明かし、le treが彼の内なる光をより深く見極めることになるだろうと告げた。

## Scene 112-117: The Procession to the Griffin
en: Singing, `*le anime beate*` lead `*Dante*` toward `*Beatrice*` at the `*beata riva*`, urging him to look forward and recall the force that first overcame him.
ja: 歌声を響かせながら、le anime beateはDanteをbeata rivaに待つBeatriceへと導き、前を見据え、かつて彼を捉えたあの力を思い出すよう促した。

## Scene 118-123: The Splendor of the Griffin
en: At the *beata riva*, *al petto del grifon*, *Beatrice* gazes into his eyes with a burning intensity, her eyes reflecting the radiance of the double beast like the sun in a mirror.
ja: beata rivaにて、grifonの胸に寄り添うBeatriceは、燃えるような情熱をもってその目を見つめている。彼女の瞳は、鏡に映る太陽のように、その二身の獣の光輝を反射させていた。

## Scene 124-126: Dante's Wonder
en: At the *beata riva*, *al petto del grifon*, *Dante* invites the mentioned *lettore* to imagine his wonder at seeing a vision remain still yet transform within its own reflection.
ja: 祝福された岸、グリフォンの胸元にて、Danteは、ある幻影が静止したままでありながら自らの鏡像の中で変容する様を見たときの驚きを、前述のlettoreに想像させる。

## Scene 127-132: The Celestial Dance
en: At the *beata riva*, *al petto del grifon*, *Dante*'s soul tastes a divine nourishment that both satisfies and awakens its longing for a higher state, while *l'altre tre* advance in an angelic dance.
ja: 至福の岸、グリフォンの胸元にて、Danteの魂は神聖な滋養を味わい、充足しながらも同時に、より高き境地への憧憬を呼び覚まされる。その傍らでは l'altre tre が、天使のような舞いとともに進んでゆく。

## Scene 133-138: The Song of the Nymphs
en: At the *beata riva*, *al petto del grifon*, *le quattro anime beate* sing to *Beatrice*, pleading with her to look upon *Dante* and grant him the grace of hearing her voice.
ja: 至福の岸辺、グリフォンの胸元にて、le quattro anime beate は Beatrice に向かって歌い、Dante に目を向け、彼女の声を聞くという恩寵を彼に授けるよう懇願した。

## Scene 139-145: Hymn to Eternal Light
en: At the *beata riva*, *al petto del grifon*, a song praises *Beatrice* as eternal light, recalling how *Dante* grew pale in his earthly attempts to describe her beauty before she was released from her mortal body.
ja: 祝福された岸辺、グリフォンの胸において、ある歌がBeatriceを永遠なる光として讃えていた。そこでは、彼女がこの世の身を離れる前、Danteが地上の言葉でその美しさを描き出そうとしていかに青ざめたかが語られていた。
