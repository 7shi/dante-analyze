# Canto 31 — The Sphinx's Instruction and the Celestial Dance

## Scene 1-6: The Sphinx's Accusation
en: *Beatrice* speaks sharply to *Dante* from the *coscia del carro*, demanding that he provide a confession to match the accusation she has brought against him.
ja: Beatriceは車の腿からDanteに鋭く言い放ち、自らが突きつけた告発に見合う告白をせよと迫った。

## Scene 7-15: Dante's Hesitant Response
en: On the *coscia del carro*, *Dante* is so overcome by confusion and fear that his voice fails him, leading *Beatrice* to demand his answer while his memories are still clear. He manages only a faint "yes" that *Beatrice* must read from his lips.
ja: 車の側面にいたDanteは、あまりの混乱と恐怖に声を失い、記憶が鮮明なうちに答えよとBeatriceに迫られた。彼はかろうじて唇を動かして「はい」と答え、Beatriceはそれを読み取った。

## Scene 16-21: The Burst of Sorrow
en: On the *coscia del carro*, *Dante* breaks under a heavy burden like a crossbow snapping from too much tension, erupting in tears and sighs as his voice fails him.
ja: 車の傍らで、Danteは張り詰めすぎた弓が弾けるように重圧に耐えかねて崩れ落ち、声を失い、涙と嘆きを激しく溢れさせた。

## Scene 22-30: The Sphinx's Inquiry
en: On the *coscia del carro*, *Beatrice* asks *Dante* what obstacles or chains hindered his desire for the Good and what advantages *li altri* possessed that allowed them to surpass him.
ja: 御車の傍らで、BeatriceはDanteに、いかなる障害や鎖が善への憧憬を妨げたのか、そしてli altriが彼を追い越せたのはどのような利点があったからなのかと問いかけた。

## Scene 31-36: The Confession of Sin
en: On the *coscia del carro*, *Dante* struggles to find his voice and, weeping, confesses that the false pleasure of worldly things led him astray once a certain face was hidden from him.
ja: coscia del carroの上で、Danteは声を絞り出すのに苦しみ、ある面影を失ったことで世俗の偽りの快楽に惑わされ、道を誤ったのだと涙ながらに告白した。

## Scene 37-48: The Command to Listen
en: On the *coscia del carro*, *Beatrice* tells *Dante* that while *Dio* already knows all faults, his heartfelt confession softens the judgment. She commands him to stop weeping and listen to how he must now move forward.
ja: 車の腿の上で、BeatriceはDanteに、Dioはあらゆる過ちをすでに知っておられるが、心からの告白がその審判を和らげるのだと語った。そして、涙を止めて、これからいかにして前へ進むべきかを聞くようにと命じた。

## Scene 49-63: The Vanity of Earthly Beauty
en: On the *coscia del carro*, *Beatrice* argues that because her own earthly beauty was the highest possible mortal pleasure, *Dante* should have used her death as a warning against the vanity of the world rather than continuing to pursue lesser worldly lures.
ja: 車の腿の上で、Beatriceは、地上における己の美しさが人間にとって至上の快楽であったのだから、Danteは劣った世俗の誘惑に耽るのではなく、彼女の死をこの世の虚しさを悟るための警鐘とすべきだったと説いた。

## Scene 64-69: A Call to Courage
en: On the *coscia del carro*, *Dante* stands bowed and repentant like shamed *fanciulli*; *Beatrice* commands him to lift his head to hear her, warning that looking upon her will only increase his pain.
ja: 車の太ももの上に立つ Dante は、恥じらう fanciulli のように頭を垂れて悔恨に沈んでいた。Beatrice は、彼女を直視すればただ苦しみが増すだけだと警告しながら、己が声を聴くために顔を上げるよう彼に命じる。

## Scene 70-75: The Simile of the Stripped Tree
en: On the *coscia del carro*, *Dante* likens himself to a tree stripped bare by the winds of *Iarba* or by *men di resistenza*, and as *Beatrice* compels him to look up, he recognizes the stinging truth of her argument.
ja: 車の側面に座したDanteは、自らをIarbaの風やmen di resistenzaによって剥き出しにされた木に例えていた。だが、Beatriceに顔を上げさせられたとき、彼は彼女の主張に潜む痛烈な真実を悟った。

## Scene 76-84: The Vision of Beatrice
en: On the *coscia del carro*, *Dante* lifts his face and sees *Beatrice* gazing toward the mentioned *Cristo*, noting that she now appears more radiant than ever before.
ja: 車の腿に身を預けたDanteが顔を上げると、そこにはCristoを見つめるBeatriceの姿があり、彼女がかつてないほどに眩い輝きを放っていることがわかった。

## Scene 85-90: The Sting of Repentance
en: On the *coscia del carro*, *Dante* feels the sharp sting of repentance, sensing that the love of *Beatrice* has become an enemy by exposing his faults. Overwhelmed by this recognition, *Dante* collapses in defeat while *Beatrice* observes him as the cause of his sorrow.
ja: 車の縁で、DanteはBeatriceの愛が自らの過ちを暴き出す敵へと変わったことを悟り、鋭い悔悟の痛みに苛まれる。その認識に打ちひしがれて崩れ落ちるDanteを、Beatriceは自らが彼の悲しみの源となって見つめていた。

## Scene 91-96: Crossing the River
en: As *Dante* recovers his strength on the *coscia del carro*, *Beatrice* warns him to fear her and pulls him into the river up to his throat. She then glides effortlessly across the water, towing him behind her.
ja: `Dante`が車の腿の部分で力を取り戻すと、`Beatrice`は自分を恐れるようにと彼に警告し、喉まで川へと引きずり込んだ。そして彼女は、彼を後ろに曳きながら、軽やかに水面を滑っていった。

## Scene 97-105: Arrival at the Blessed Shore
en: At the *beata riva*, *Beatrice* embraces *Dante* and dips him beneath the water to purify him. She then leads him to *le quattro belle*, who surround him with their arms.
ja: 祝福された岸辺にて、BeatriceはDanteを抱きしめ、彼を浄めるために水の下へと沈めた。それから彼女は彼をle quattro belleのもとへ導き、彼女たちは彼をその腕で包み込んだ。

## Scene 106-111: The Nymphs' Identity
en: At the `*beata riva*`, the `*ninfe*` reveal that they are celestial stars who served as handmaidens to the mentioned `*Beatrice*` before her earthly descent, noting that `*le tre*` will examine his internal light with even greater depth.
ja: 福なる岸辺にて、`ninfe`たちは、自らが天の星であり、地上に降り立つ前の`Beatrice`に侍女として仕えていたことを明かし、`le tre`が彼の内なる光をさらに深く見極めることになると告げた。

## Scene 112-117: The Procession to the Griffin
en: At the *beata riva*, *le anime beate* sing as they lead *Dante* toward *Beatrice*. They command *Dante* to look ahead without hesitation, having positioned him before the sight that once overwhelmed him.
ja: beata rivaにて、le anime beateは歌いながらDanteをBeatriceへと導く。かつて彼を圧倒した光景の前に彼を立たせ、彼らはDanteに、躊躇わず前を見るよう命じた。

## Scene 118-123: The Splendor of the Griffin
en: At the *beata riva*, *al petto del grifon*, *Beatrice* gazes into his eyes with a thousand fiery desires, and he sees the double beast reflected in her eyes like the sun in a mirror.
ja: 祝福された岸辺、グリフォンの胸元で、Beatriceは千もの燃えるような情念を湛えて彼の瞳を見つめ、彼は鏡に映る太陽のように、彼女の瞳の中に二頭の獣が映っているのを見た。

## Scene 124-126: Dante's Wonder
en: At the `*beata riva*`, `*al petto del grifon*`, `*Dante*` invites the `*lettore*` to consider his astonishment at how a reflected image remains still while simultaneously transforming.
ja: `beata riva`の`al petto del grifon`にて、`Dante`は`lettore`に、映し出された像が静止しながら同時に変容するという不思議への驚きを考察するよう促している。

## Scene 127-132: The Celestial Dance
en: At the *beata riva*, *al petto del grifon*, *Dante*'s soul is filled with joyful wonder as it tastes a divine food that satisfies while creating further thirst. Meanwhile, *l'altre tre* dance forward to an angelic rhythm.
ja: 祝福された岸辺、グリフォンの胸のもとで、Danteの魂は、満たされるほどにさらなる渇望を募らせる神聖な糧を味わい、歓喜に満ちた驚嘆に浸っていた。一方、l'altre treは天使のリズムに合わせて、前へと舞い踊る。

## Scene 133-138: The Song of the Nymphs
en: At the *beata riva*, *al petto del grifon*, *le quattro anime beate* sing to *Beatrice*, pleading with her to turn her holy eyes toward the mentioned *Dante* and unveil the beauty of her voice to him.
ja: 福なる岸辺、グリフォンの胸元にて、le quattro anime beate は Beatrice に向かって歌い、その聖なる眼差しを Dante へと向け、麗しき声を彼に届けてほしいと切に請い願った。

## Scene 139-145: Hymn to Eternal Light
en: At the *beata riva*, *al petto del grifon*, *Beatrice* is praised as a splendor of eternal light in a hymn recalling how *Dante* once struggled to capture her beauty in poetry and how she eventually released herself from her mortal existence.
ja: 至福の岸辺、グリフォンの胸元にて、Beatriceは永遠なる光の輝きとして讃えられる。その賛歌は、かつてDanteが彼女の美を詩に捉えようと苦心したこと、そして彼女がいかにして現世の生から解き放たれたかを回想するものであった。
