# Canto 27 — The Entrance into the Fire and the Ascent to the Earthly Paradise

## Scene 1-6: The Solar Metaphor
en: As the sun sets in a way that recalls where `*Cristo*` shed his blood, `*l'angel di Dio*` joyfully appears on the `*scala*` to `*Dante*`, `*Virgilio*`, and `*Stazio*`.
ja: `Cristo`が血を流した地を彷彿とさせるように日が沈みゆく頃、`l'angel di Dio`が歓喜に満ちて階段の上に現れ、`Dante`、`Virgilio`、そして`Stazio`の前に姿を現した。

## Scene 7-13: The Angel's Command
en: *In su la riva*, *l'angelo* sings a divine verse and tells *Dante*, *Virgilio*, and *Stazio* that they cannot proceed without first entering the fire. He commands them to enter the flames and listen to the singing from the other side.
ja: 岸辺にて、l'angeloは神聖な詩を歌い、Dante, Virgilio, Stazioに、まずは火の中に入らなければ先へは進めないと告げた。そして彼らに、炎の中へと入り、対岸から聞こえる歌声に耳を傾けるよう命じた。

## Scene 14-18: Dante's Terror
en: *In su la riva*, *Dante* is seized by terror and extends his hands, imagining the fire and remembering past horrors.
ja: 岸辺にて、Danteは恐怖に襲われ、猛火を想像し、過去の惨劇を思い出しながら両手を伸ばした。

## Scene 19-32: Virgil's Reassurance
en: *In su la riva*, *Virgilio* and *Stazio* turn toward *Dante*, and *Virgilio* calms his terror by arguing that if he could lead him safely past *Gerïon*, he can certainly do so now that they are closer to *Dio*. He challenges *Dante* to test the harmlessness of the fire with his own clothing and commands him to enter securely.
ja: 岸辺にて、VirgilioとStazioがDanteへと向き直った。Virgilioは、かつてGerïonを安全に通り抜けさせることができたのだから、Dioに近づいた今となってはなおさら可能であると説いて彼の恐怖を鎮め、自らの衣で火の無害さを確かめるよう促すと、自信を持って中へ進むよう命じた。

## Scene 33-36: The Wall of Separation
en: *In su la riva*, *Dante* remains frozen in place, so a slightly troubled *Virgilio* tells him that this wall is the only thing separating him from *Bëatrice*.
ja: 岸辺に立ち尽くすDanteに、少し困惑した様子のVirgilioが、この壁だけが彼とBëatriceを隔てているのだと告げた。

## Scene 37-42: Dante's Conversion
en: *In su la riva*, *Dante*'s stubbornness softens, recalling how the mentioned *Piramo* looked upon the mentioned *Tisbe*, and he turns back to *Virgilio*.
ja: 岸辺にて、Danteの頑なな心は、PiramoがTisbeを見つめたあの様子を思い出して和らぎ、彼は再びVirgilioへと向き直った。

## Scene 43-48: Entering the Fire
en: *In su la riva*, *Virgilio* smiles at *Dante*’s change of heart and asks if he really intended to stay behind. He then leads the way into the fire, calling for *Stazio* to follow him after their long separation.
ja: 岸辺にて、VirgilioはDanteの心変わりを微笑み、本当にここに残るつもりだったのかと問いかけた。それから彼は火の中へと先導し、長い別離を経てStazioに自分に続くよう呼びかけた。

## Scene 49-54: The Intensity of the Flame
en: *In su la riva*, *Dante* is so overwhelmed by the fire's intensity that he would plunge into boiling glass for relief, but *Virgilio* comforts him by speaking of *Beatrice* and imagining he can already see her eyes.
ja: su la rivaにて、Danteは火の激しさにあまりに圧倒され、救いを求めて煮えたぎるガラスに身を投じたいとさえ願ったが、VirgilioはBeatriceについて語り、その瞳がすでに目に浮かぶかのように説いて彼を慰めた。

## Scene 55-63: The Heavenly Voice
en: *In su la riva*, a singing voice guides *Dante* and *Virgilio* out of the fire and welcomes them as the blessed of *Patris*. The accompanying blinding light overwhelms *Dante* and urges them to hasten their pace before the evening darkens.
ja: 岸辺に響く歌声がDanteとVirgilioを炎から導き出し、Patrisに祝福されし者として彼らを迎え入れた。同時に降り注ぐ眩い光がDanteを圧倒し、二人は夕闇が深まる前に歩みを速めるよう促された。

## Scene 64-69: The Upward Climb
en: *Dante*, *Virgilio*, and *Stazio* climb the path *per entro 'l sasso*, and as the sun sinks, *Dante* feels *l'ombra* being extinguished behind them.
ja: Dante, Virgilio, そしてStazioは岩の間を縫う道を登り、陽が沈みゆくなか、Danteは背後でl'ombraが消えゆくのを感じた。

## Scene 70-75: The Evening Rest
en: As night falls, *Dante* and *Virgilio* each find a *grado* to serve as a bed, for the climb has exhausted their power to ascend further.
ja: 夜の帳が降りると、DanteとVirgilioはそれぞれ寝床代わりの段に身を横たえた。登攀の疲れにより、もはやそれ以上登る力は残っていなかった。

## Scene 76-87: The Simile of the Shepherds
en: Resting in the *alta grotta*, *Dante*, *Virgilio*, and *Stazio* remain peaceful, and *Dante* compares their state to the watchful protection of a mentioned *pastor*.
ja: 高い洞窟で休息するDante, Virgilio, Stazioの三人は平穏に満ちており、Danteはそのありようを、あるpastorによる細やかな保護になぞらえた。

## Scene 88-93: The Arrival of Sleep
en: In the *alta grotta*, *Dante* contemplates the bright stars until he falls into a sleep that often possesses foreknowledge of what is to come.
ja: alta grottaにて、Danteは輝く星々を凝視し、やがて、しばしば来るべきことの予兆を孕む眠りに落ちた。

## Scene 94-108: The Dream of Lia
en: In the `*alta grotta*`, *Dante* dreams of a beautiful *Lia* gathering flowers to weave a garland for herself. *Lia* sings that while she finds joy in the work of adorning herself, the mentioned *Rachel* prefers to spend her time gazing in a mirror.
ja: 高い洞窟の中で、Danteは、自らのために花冠を編もうと花を摘む美しいLiaの夢を見る。Liaは、自分は身を飾ることに喜びを見出すが、例のRachelは鏡を眺めて時を過ごすことを好むのだと歌う。

## Scene 109-114: The Dawn of Awakening
en: In the *alta grotta*, the pre-dawn light drives away the darkness and awakens *Dante*. He rises to find that *Virgilio* and *Stazio* are already standing.
ja: alta grottaに夜明け前の光が差し込んで闇を払い、Danteは目を覚ました。身を起こすと、VirgilioとStazioはすでに立っていた。

## Scene 115-120: Virgil's Comforting Words
en: In the *alta grotta*, *Virgilio* tells *Dante* that the peace sought by *mortali* will finally satisfy his longing today. *Dante* finds these words more pleasing than any he has ever heard.
ja: 高き洞窟にて、VirgilioはDanteに、mortaliが追い求める平安が今日ついにその切望を満たすだろうと告げた。Danteにとって、それはこれまで耳にしたどの言葉よりも心地よい響きであった。

## Scene 121-123: The Impulse to Ascend
en: In the *alta grotta*, *Dante* is seized by such an overwhelming desire to ascend that, with every step, he feels as if wings were growing upon him.
ja: alta grottaにおいて、Danteは抗いがたいほど強烈な上昇への渇望に駆られ、一歩踏み出すたびに、まるで自らの身に翼が生えてくるかのように感じた。

## Scene 124-132: The Limit of Virgil's Guidance
en: Having reached the *grado superno*, *Virgilio* looks at *Dante* and explains that since he led him here through intellect and art, he can go no further. He tells *Dante* to now let his own pleasure lead the way.
ja: 至高の階位に到達したVirgilioはDanteを見つめ、知性と芸術によって彼をここまで導いた自分は、これ以上先へは行けないのだと説明した。そして、ここからは自らの喜びに従って進むようにとDanteに告げた。

## Scene 133-138: The Beauty of the Paradise
en: On *la terra*, *Virgilio* tells *Dante* to admire the sunlight and the flowers while awaiting *Beatrice*, recalling how her tears had prompted *Virgilio*'s guidance. He suggests that *Dante* may either sit or wander among the greenery until *Beatrice* arrives.
ja: 地上にて、VirgilioはBeatriceを待つ間、陽光と花々を愛でるようDanteに伝え、彼女の涙が自らを導き手たらしめたことを回想した。Beatriceが到着するまで、緑の中で座って過ごすか、あるいは散策していてもよいだろうと彼は勧めた。

## Scene 139-142: The Granting of Free Will
en: On *la terra*, *Virgilio* tells *Dante* that he no longer needs to await his guidance, for his will is now free and healthy. He cautions *Dante* not to let his will deviate from reason, as *Virgilio* has now effectively crowned and anointed him.
ja: la terraにて、VirgilioはDanteに、その意志は今や自由で健やかであるため、もはや導きを待つ必要はないと告げた。そして、実質的に彼に戴冠し塗油を施した今、決してその意志を理性から逸らしてはならないと、VirgilioはDanteに諭した。
