# Canto 12 — The Descent into the Valley of Violence and the Encounter with the Centaurs

## Scene 1-15: The Ruin and the Minotaur
en: *Dante* and *Virgilio* arrive at *lo loco ov' a scender la riva venimmo*, a steep and ruined cliff. Upon seeing them, the *Minotauro*—conceived through *la falsa vacca*—bites himself in a fit of rage.
ja: DanteとVirgilioは、岸を降りて切り立ち崩れかけた崖に辿り着いた。二人の姿を見たMinotauroは――la falsa vaccaによって産まれた彼は――激昂のあまり、自らの身を噛んだ。

## Scene 16-21: Virgil's Rebuke
en: At *lo loco ov' a scender la riva venimmo*, *Virgilio* shouts at *Minotauro*, asserting that he is not *Teseo* and that *Dante* has not been trained by *la tua sorella* but has come to see the punishments.
ja: 岸辺を下りて至ったその場所で、VirgilioはMinotauroに向かって、自分はTeseoではないこと、そしてDanteはla tua sorellaに導かれたのではなく、ただ罰を目の当たりにするためにやって来たのだと叫んだ。

## Scene 22-27: The Frenzy of the Bull
en: At *lo loco ov' a scender la riva venimmo*, *Dante* observes the *Minotauro* leaping erratically like a mortally wounded bull. Seizing the moment, *Virgilio* urges *Dante* to hurry to the gap and descend while the beast is in a frenzy.
ja: 「lo loco ov' a scender la riva venimmo」の場面で、Danteは致命傷を負った雄牛のごとく不規則に跳ね回るMinotauroを目にする。その機を逃さず、Virgilioは怪物が狂乱に陥っている間に急いで隙間へ向かい、降りるようDanteに促した。

## Scene 28-30: The Descent
en: *Dante* and *Virgilio* descend *lo scarco di quelle pietre*, where the stones frequently shift beneath *Dante*'s feet under his weight.
ja: DanteとVirgilioがその石の斜面を降りていくと、Danteの体重に押され、足元の石がたびたびずれた。

## Scene 31-45: The Earthquake and the Fall
en: At *lo scarco di quelle pietre*, *Virgilio* tells the pensive *Dante* that these ruins did not exist during his last visit, but collapsed during a massive earthquake that occurred shortly before the descent of *Lucifero*.
ja: それらの岩の急斜面にて、Virgilioは物思いに沈むDanteに、この廃墟は前回の訪れには存在せず、Luciferoが降臨する直前に起きた大地震によって崩落したものだと語った。

## Scene 46-48: The River of Blood
en: At *lo scarco di quelle pietre*, *Dante* is told to look down toward the valley, as they approach the river of boiling blood where those who committed violence against others are punished.
ja: lo scarco di quelle pietreに至り、他者に暴力を振るった者が罰せられる煮えたぎる血の川へと近づくなか、Danteは谷の方を見下ろすよう促された。

## Scene 49-51: Dante's Moral Reflection
en: At *lo scarco di quelle pietre*, *Dante* reflects on how blind greed and foolish anger spur people during their short lives and lead to their eternal sacrifice.
ja: それらの石を降ろす場面で、Danteは、盲目的な強欲と愚かな怒りが人の短き生をいかに駆り立て、そして永遠の犠牲へと導くのかに思いを馳せる。

## Scene 52-57: The Centaurs' Patrol
en: At `*lo scarco di quelle pietre*`, `*Dante*` observes a wide, curved ditch as `*Virgilio*` had mentioned, and sees `*centauri*` armed with arrows patrolling the area as they once did when hunting in the world.
ja: それらの岩が降り積もる場所で、DanteはVirgilioが述べていた通りに幅広く湾曲した溝を目にし、かつて地上で狩りをしていた時のように、矢を構えて辺りを巡回するcentauriの姿を見た。

## Scene 58-63: The Centaurs' Challenge
en: Seeing `*Dante*` and `*Virgilio*` descend `*la costa*`, three `*centauri*` separate from the `*schiera*` with bows and quivers. `*un centauro*` shouts to them to reveal what brings them there, threatening to shoot if they do not answer.
ja: `Dante` と `Virgilio` が斜面を降りてくるのが見えると、弓と矢筒を携えた三体の `centauri` が `schiera` から離れてやってきた。`un centauro` が、ここへ何用で来たのか答えよと叫び、さもなくば射抜くと彼らを脅した。

## Scene 64-66: Virgil's Strategy
en: At *la costa*, *Virgilio* tells *Dante* that they will answer *Chirón* once they are closer, remarking that *Chirón* was always too hasty.
ja: la costaにて、VirgilioはDanteに、もっと近づいてからChirónに答えようと告げ、Chirónはいつも気が急ぎすぎると付け加えた。

## Scene 67-72: Identification of the Centaurs
en: At *la costa*, *Virgilio* identifies for *Dante* the three centaurs: *Nesso*, who died because of the mentioned *Deianira*; *Chirón*, who once nourished the mentioned *Achille*; and the rage-filled *Folo*.
ja: 岸辺において、VirgilioはDanteに三人のケンタウロスを指し示した。前述のDeianiraによって命を落としたNesso、かつて前述のAchilleを育てたChirón、そして怒りに満ちたFoloである。

## Scene 73-75: The Rain of Arrows
en: At `*la costa*`, the `*centauri*` patrol in their thousands, shooting any `*l'anima*` that attempts to rise higher than its sin allows.
ja: `la costa`では数千の`centauri`が巡回し、己の罪が許す高さを超えて昇ろうとする`l'anima`を射抜いている。

## Scene 76-79: The Inspection
en: As *Dante* and *Virgilio* approach the *centauri* at *la costa*, *Chirón* nocks an arrow and draws it back past his jaws, exposing his wide mouth.
ja: DanteとVirgilioが海岸のcentauriに近づくと、Chirónは矢を番えて顎の先まで引き絞り、大きく開いた口を露わにした。

## Scene 80-82: Chiron's Suspicion
en: At *la costa*, *Chirone* asks *Nesso* and *Folo* if they notice that *Dante* moves his feet as he walks, observing that this is not how *i morti* usually behave.
ja: la costaにて、ChironeはDanteが歩く際に足を動かしていることに気づいているかとNessoとFoloに問いかけ、そのような振る舞いはi mortiの常ではないと指摘した。

## Scene 83-96: Virgil's Plea for Passage
en: On the *selvaggia strada*, *Virgilio* explains to *Chirone* that *Dante* is alive and that the mentioned *Beatrice* entrusted him with this necessary journey. He asks *Chirone* to assign *un centauro* to guide them to the crossing and carry *Dante* on its back, as he is not a spirit.
ja: 険しい道にて、VirgilioはChironeに、Danteが生きていること、そしてBeatriceがこの不可欠な旅を彼に託したことを説明した。また、Danteは霊ではないため、渡し場まで彼らを導き、その背にDanteを乗せて運ぶun centauroを配属するようChironeに求めた。

## Scene 97-99: The Order to Guide
en: On the *selvaggia strada*, *Chirone* turns to *Nesso* and orders him to go back and guide *Dante* and *Virgilio*, instructing him to halt any *altra schiera* they may encounter.
ja: selvaggia stradaにて、ChironeはNessoに向き、引き返してDanteとVirgilioを案内し、行く手に現れるいかなるaltra schieraをも制止するようにと命じた。

## Scene 100-102: The Boiling Blood
en: *Dante* and *Virgilio* move along *la proda del bollor vermiglio* escorted by *Nesso*, where *i bolliti* let out loud cries.
ja: DanteとVirgilioはNessoに導かれ、i bollitiが激しく叫ぶ朱色の沸き立つ岸辺を進んでゆく。

## Scene 103-112: The Tyrants in the Blood
en: At *la proda del bollor vermiglio*, *Dante* observes the *tiranni* immersed in blood, whom *Nesso* identifies as *Alessandro* and *Dïonisio*, the latter spoken of for the pain he caused *Cicilia*. *Nesso* also points out *Azzolino* and *Opizzo da Esti*, noting that the latter was killed by his mentioned *figliastro*.
ja: la proda del bollor vermiglioにて、Danteは血に浸るtiranniを目の当たりにする。Nessoは彼らをAlessandroとDïonisioであると告げ、後者がCiciliaに与えた苦しみに言及すると、さらにAzzolinoとOpizzo da Estiを指し示し、後者はそのfigliastroに殺されたことを語った。

## Scene 113-114: Dante's Turn
en: At *la proda del bollor vermiglio*, *Dante* turns to *Virgilio* and suggests that *Nesso* be the first in their order, followed by *Virgilio* and then himself.
ja: 緋色の沸騰する岸辺にて、DanteはVirgilioに向き直り、Nessoを先頭とし、次にVirgilio、そして最後に自分が続く順序にすることを提案した。

## Scene 115-120: The Shadow of the Thames
en: At *la proda del bollor vermiglio*, *Nesso* halts before *gente* submerged in the boiling blood and points out *un'ombra*. He declares that *un'ombra* pierced the heart of *Dio* and that the blood of that heart still drips.
ja: 沸き立つ深紅の岸辺にて、Nessoは沸騰する血に浸かるgenteの前で足を止め、あるun'ombraを指し示した。彼は、un'ombraがDioの心臓を貫いたため、今もなおその心臓から血が滴っているのだと告げた。

## Scene 121-123: The Severed Heads
en: At *la proda del bollor vermiglio*, *Dante* observes *gente* who keep their heads and necks above the river, recognizing many among them.
ja: 真紅に沸き立つ岸辺で、Danteは川の上に頭と首だけを出しているgenteを眺め、そのなかの多くの者を識別した。

## Scene 124-126: The Rising Tide
en: The blood gradually receded until it reached only the feet, and then the ditch was exited at *la proda del bollor vermiglio*.
ja: 血は次第に引き、ついには足元までしか浸らなくなった。そして la proda del bollor vermiglio に至り、その溝を脱した。

## Scene 127-132: The Depths of Tyranny
en: At `*la proda del bollor vermiglio*`, `*Nesso*` explains to `*Dante*` that the boiling blood shallows on their side but deepens on the opposite side until it reaches the lowest depths.
ja: `la proda del bollor vermiglio`にて、`Nesso`は`Dante`に、沸き立つ血は彼らのいる側では浅いが、対岸に向かって深まり、やがて最深部へと至るのだと説明した。

## Scene 133-138: The Scourge of Attila
en: At *la proda del bollor vermiglio*, divine justice eternally extracts tears from *Attila*, *Pirro*, and *Sesto*. This same punishment befalls *Rinier da Corneto* and *Rinier Pazzo* for the wars they waged in the streets.
ja: 真紅に煮え立つ岸辺にて、神の正義はAttila、Pirro、そしてSestoから永遠に涙を絞り出している。市街で戦を繰り広げたRinier da CornetoとRinier Pazzoにも、同じ罰が下されていた。

## Scene 139-139: Departure
en: At *la proda del bollor vermiglio*, *Dante*, *Virgilio*, and *Nesso* turn around and retrace their steps through the shallows.
ja: 真紅に沸き立つ岸辺で、Dante、Virgilio、Nessoは向きを変え、浅瀬を辿って引き返した。
