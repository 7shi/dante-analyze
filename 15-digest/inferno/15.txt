# Canto 15 — The Encounter with Brunetto Latino

## Scene 1-12: The Simile of the Flooding Banks
en: *Dante* and *Virgilio* walk along the *margini* in the *bosco*, where the banks protect them from the fire. These barriers are compared to those built by the *Fiamminghi*, though they are not as vast as if *Dio* had designed them.
ja: DanteとVirgilioは森の縁に沿って歩む。彼らを火から守るその土手は、Fiamminghiが築いた障壁に比肩するものの、Dioが設計したほどに広大なものではなかった。

## Scene 13-21: The Approaching Procession
en: *Dante* and *Virgilio* have moved far from the *bosco* and are now on *l'argine*, where they encounter *d'anime una schiera*. These souls squinted at them as if observing *uno altro* under a new moon or as *l vecchio sartor* peers at a needle's eye.
ja: 森を遠く離れ、岸辺へと至ったDanteとVirgilioは、そこでd'anime una schieraに出会った。その魂たちは、新月の下でuno altroを凝視するか、あるいはl vecchio sartorが針の穴を覗き込むかのように、目を細めて二人を眺めた。

## Scene 22-30: The Recognition of Dante
en: On *l'argine*, a member of *cotal famiglia* recognizes *Dante* and seizes him in surprise. *Dante* peers at the man's scorched face and recognizes him as *ser Brunetto*.
ja: 堤防の上で、cotal famigliaの一人がDanteに気づき、不意に彼を捕らえた。Danteがその男の焼けただれた顔を凝視すると、それがser Brunettoであることに気づいた。

## Scene 31-42: Brunetto's Instructions
en: On *l'argine*, *Brunetto Latino* asks to walk with *Dante*, and *Dante* agrees provided *Virgilio* permits it. *Brunetto Latino* warns that any of *qual di questa greggia* who stops will be scorched for a hundred years, so he urges *Dante* to keep moving while he follows briefly.
ja: 岸辺にて、Brunetto LatinoがDanteに同行を願い、DanteはVirgilioが許すのであればと同意した。Brunetto Latinoは、qual di questa greggiaの者が立ち止まれば百年にわたって焼かれると警告し、自らが短く付き添う間、Danteに歩き続けるよう促した。

## Scene 43-54: Dante's Explanation of his Descent
en: On *la strada*, *Dante* walks with his head bowed like *l'uom* mentioned as reverent, prompting *Brunetto Latino* to ask what brought him here and who *Virgilio* is. *Dante* explains that *Virgilio* found him lost and guided him along this path.
ja: 道の上で、Danteが敬虔なl'uomのように深く頭を垂れて歩く姿を見たBrunetto Latinoは、何が彼をここへ導いたのか、そしてVirgilioとは誰なのかと問いかけた。Danteは、迷っていた自分をVirgilioが見つけ出し、この道へと導いてくれたのだと説明した。

## Scene 55-60: The Promise of Destiny
en: On *la strada*, *Brunetto Latino* tells *Dante* that he will reach a glorious destination if he follows his star. He adds that he would have supported *Dante* in his work had he not died, noting how favored *Dante* is.
ja: 道すがら、Brunetto Latinoは、自らの星に従えば栄光ある目的地に到達できるだろうとDanteに告げた。そして、もし自分が死んでいなければその業を支えていたはずだと付け加え、Danteがいかに恵まれているかを説いた。

## Scene 61-69: The Warning Against Fiesole
en: On *la strada*, *Dante* is warned that *i fiorentini* will become his enemies due to their greed and pride. He is urged to distance himself from the customs of *i fiorentini*.
ja: 道すがら、Danteは、i fiorentiniが強欲と傲慢さゆえに自らの敵となるだろうと警告され、彼らの風習から距離を置くよう促された。

## Scene 70-78: The Prophecy of the Holy Seed
en: On *la strada*, *Dante* is told that he will be honored and kept safe from those who hunger for him, while the corrupt are urged to consume only themselves.
ja: la stradaにて、Danteは、自らを渇望する者たちから守られ、敬われることになると告げられる。一方、堕落した者たちは、ただ己のみを食らえと促される。

## Scene 79-87: Dante's Tribute to his Teacher
en: On *la strada*, *Dante* expresses his deep gratitude to *Brunetto Latino*, recalling the paternal image of the man who taught him how *l'uom* might become eternal. He vows that as long as he lives, he will honor *Brunetto Latino* in his writings.
ja: 道の上で、Danteはl'uomがいかにして永遠になり得るかを説いた父のような彼の面影を回想し、Brunetto Latinoに深い感謝を捧げた。そして、生きている限り、自らの著作においてBrunetto Latinoを称え続けることを誓った。

## Scene 88-96: Acceptance of Fortune
en: On *la strada*, *Dante* tells *Brunetto Latino* that he is recording these words to share with *Beatrice* if he ever reaches her. He declares his resignation to his fate, noting that the world turns as it will and *l villan* continues to toil.
ja: 道すがら、DanteはBrunetto Latinoに、いつかBeatriceに辿り着いた際に伝えるため、今これらの言葉を記録しているのだと語った。彼は自らの運命への諦念を口にし、世界はただあるがままに回り続け、l villanは絶えず苦役に励むのだと述べた。

## Scene 97-99: Virgil's Instruction
en: On *la gota*, *Virgilio* turns back to look at *Dante* and instructs him to listen carefully to the signal.
ja: la gotaにて、VirgilioはDanteを振り返り、合図を注意深く聞くようにと指示した。

## Scene 100-105: Dante's Inquiry
en: On *la gota*, *Dante* asks *Ser Brunetto Latino* about the identity of *li suoi compagni* who are most distinguished. *Ser Brunetto Latino* replies that knowing about him is enough, as the time available is too short to name the others.
ja: その滴において、DanteはSer Brunetto Latinoに、最も著名なli suoi compagniが誰であるかを尋ねた。Ser Brunetto Latinoは、他の者たちの名を挙げるには時間が足りないため、自分について知るだけで十分であると答えた。

## Scene 106-114: The Catalog of Scholars
en: On *la gota*, one of the *cherci* informs *Dante* that all the *cherci* are sinners, specifically naming *Priscian* and *Francesco d'Accorso* and mentioning the presence of *Adriano V*.
ja: その雫の上で、cherciの一人がDanteに、cherciはみな罪人であると告げ、具体的にPriscianとFrancesco d'Accorsoの名を挙げ、さらにAdriano Vの存在についても言及した。

## Scene 115-120: The Interrupted Farewell
en: On *la gota*, *Dante* wishes to speak further but must depart as new *gente* approach. He bids a final, affectionate farewell to *Ser Brunetto Latino*, whose guidance he still treasures.
ja: その場所で、Danteはさらに語らいたいと願ったが、新たなgenteが近づいてきたため、立ち去らねばならなかった。彼は、今なおその導きを大切にしているSer Brunetto Latinoに、親愛の情を込めて最後の別れを告げた。

## Scene 121-124: The Final Simile
en: On *la gota*, *Brunetto Latino* turns away, appearing like *quelli che vince* rather than one who loses.
ja: その雫の上で、Brunetto Latinoは身を翻した。その姿は敗者というよりも、むしろquelli che vinceのように見えた。
