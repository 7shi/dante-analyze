# Canto 10 — The Encounter with Farinata and the Shadows of Dis

## Scene 1-9: Dante’s Inquiry
en: Following *Virgilio* along a *secreto calle*, *Dante* asks *Virgilio* if *li empi* may be seen since the lids of their tombs are open.
ja: Virgilioに続いて秘密の道を歩きながら、Danteは、墓の蓋が開いているのでli empiが見えるのではないかとVirgilioに尋ねた。

## Scene 10-18: The Epicurean’s Reply
en: While on the *secreto calle*, the *Eretici* explain to *Dante* that they will be sealed once more when they return to their bodies. They describe this area as the cemetery of *Epicuro* and *tutti suoi seguaci*, promising *Dante* that his inquiry will soon be satisfied.
ja: 秘密の道を歩きながら、Ereticiは自らの身体に戻る際に再び封印されることをDanteに説明した。彼らはこの場所をEpicuroとtutti suoi seguaciの墓地であると伝え、Danteの疑問も間もなく解消されるだろうと約束した。

## Scene 19-27: Virgil’s Admonition
en: On the *secreto calle*, *Dante* tells *Virgilio* he has a few words left to say, and *l'anima epicurea* is mentioned as welcoming the pilgrim for his noble speech.
ja: 秘密の道にて、DanteはVirgilioにまだ語るべき言葉があることを伝え、その気高い言葉にl'anima epicureaが巡礼者を歓迎したと記されている。

## Scene 28-33: The Voice from the Tomb
en: As a voice sounds from a tomb on the *secreto calle*, *Dante* moves closer to *Virgilio* in fear. *Virgilio* directs *Dante*'s attention to *Farinata*, who has risen upright from the waist up.
ja: 秘密の路地の墓から声が聞こえてくると、Danteは恐れてVirgilioに身を寄せた。VirgilioがDanteの視線を向けさせた先には、腰から上を突き出して立ち上がったFarinataの姿があった。

## Scene 34-39: The Defiance of Farinata
en: *Dante* gazes at *Farinata*, who rises within *le sepulture* with an air of defiance. *Virgilio* pushes *Dante* toward him and tells *Farinata* to be concise in his speech.
ja: Danteは、不遜な面持ちで墓の中から立ち上がるFarinataを凝視する。VirgilioはDanteを彼の方へと押しやり、Farinataに簡潔に話すよう促す。

## Scene 40-48: Farinata’s Inquiry
en: At the *piè de la sua tomba*, *Farinata* asks *Dante* to name his *li maggior*, and upon receiving the answer, he declares that he and his *primi* were fiercely opposed to those *li maggior*, whom he dispersed on two occasions.
ja: 墓の足元にて、FarinataはDanteにli maggiorの名を問うた。その答えを得ると、彼は自分とprimiがli maggiorと激しく対立しており、二度にわたって彼らを追い散らしたのだと宣言した。

## Scene 49-51: Dante’s Rebuttal
en: At the *piè de la sua tomba*, *Dante* replies to *Farinata* that while the *Guelfi* returned twice from exile, the *Ghibellini* never mastered that art.
ja: 墓の足もとにて、DanteはFarinataに、Guelfiは二度も追放から帰還したが、Ghibelliniはその術を身につけることはなかったと答えた。

## Scene 52-60: The Emergence of the Shadow
en: At the *piè de la sua tomba* in *le sepulture*, *un'ombra* rises and looks around to see if *Dante* is alone, then asks why he is there and where *Cavalcante della Scala* is.
ja: 墓地の彼の墓の足もとで、un'ombra が起き上がり、Dante が一人であるかを確認するように辺りを見回すと、なぜここにいるのか、そして Cavalcante della Scala はどこにいるのかと問いかけた。

## Scene 61-66: The Reference to Guido
en: At the *piè de la sua tomba* in *le sepulture*, *Dante* tells *il padre di Cavalcante della Scala* that he is being led by *Virgilio*, referencing *Guido da Montefeltro*. He explains that the soul's words and the penalty had already revealed the identity of *il padre di Cavalcante della Scala* to him.
ja: 埋葬所の墓の足もとで、DanteはGuido da Montefeltroに言及しながら、自らがVirgilioに導かれていることをil padre di Cavalcante della Scalaに告げた。その魂の言葉と罰によって、il padre di Cavalcante della Scalaの正体はすでに明らかになっていたのだと、彼は説明した。

## Scene 67-72: The Shadow’s Grief
en: At *piè de la sua tomba* in *le sepulture*, *l'ombra* suddenly rises to ask *Dante* if *Cavalcante della Scala* still lives, but then collapses back into the tomb and vanishes after noticing *Dante*'s hesitation.
ja: 墓地の墓の足元から、l'ombraが不意に起き上がり、Cavalcante della Scalaがまだ生きているのかをDanteに尋ねた。しかし、Danteの躊躇いに気づいた途端、再び墓の中へと崩れ落ちるようにして姿を消した。

## Scene 73-84: Farinata’s Question on Impiety
en: At *piè de la sua tomba* in *le sepulture*, *Farinata* remains stoic, declaring that the skill of the *Guelfi* torments him more than his punishment. He predicts that before *la donna* is renewed fifty times, *Dante* will feel the weight of that same art, and he asks why the *Guelfi* are so impious toward his people.
ja: 墓地に眠る自らの墓の足元で、Farinataは毅然とした態度を崩さず、課せられた罰よりもむしろGuelfiの術こそが自分を苛んでいるのだと断言する。彼は、la donnaが五十回新しくなる前にDanteもまたその同じ術の重みを味わうことになると予言し、なぜGuelfiはこれほどまでに自らの同胞に不敬なのかと問いかけた。

## Scene 85-87: The Metaphor of the Arbia
en: At *piè de la sua tomba* in *le sepulture*, *Dante* tells *Farinata* that the memory of a great slaughter that turned a river red is recounted like a prayer in his own temple.
ja: 墓所の足もとにて、DanteはFarinataに語った。川を赤く染めた大虐殺の記憶が、自らの神殿では祈りのように語り継がれているのだと。

## Scene 88-93: Farinata’s Lament for Florence
en: At *piè de la sua tomba* in *le sepulture*, *Farinata* sighs and claims that while he was not alone in his actions, he was the only one who defended the city openly.
ja: 埋葬地の自らの墓の足もとで、Farinataは嘆息し、同様の行いに及んだ者は他にもいたが、公然と街を守ったのは自分一人だけだったと主張する。

## Scene 94-99: Dante’s Request for Prophecy
en: At *piè de la sua tomba* in *le sepulture*, *Dante* wishes peace for the mentioned *vostra semenza* and asks *Farinata* to resolve the knot confusing his mind, acknowledging that *Farinata* seems to foresee the future and perceive the present in a different way.
ja: 墓地のその墓の足元で、Danteは言及されたvostra semenzaの安寧を願い、未来を予見し現在を異なる視点で見通しているように見えるFarinataに、自らの心を惑わせる結び目を解いてほしいと請うた。

## Scene 100-108: The Limitation of Hell’s Vision
en: At *piè de la sua tomba*, *Farinata* explains to *Dante* that le anime dell'Inferno perceive distant events like *quei c'ha mala luce*, aided by light from *il sommo duce*, yet remain blind to the present. He notes that this foresight will vanish entirely once the door to the future is closed.
ja: 自らの墓のふもとにて、FarinataはDanteに、le anime dell'Infernoはil sommo duceがもたらす光に導かれ、quei c'ha mala luceのごとく遠き出来事を予見しうるが、現在は見えないのだと説いた。そして、未来の扉が閉じれば、この予見の力は完全に消え去るであろうと彼は付け加えた。

## Scene 109-114: Dante’s Apology
en: At *piè de la sua tomba*, *Dante*, stung by guilt, asks *Farinata* to inform *quel caduto* that *Cavalcante della Scala* is still among the *vivi*, explaining that his earlier silence was due to the error that *Farinata* had just resolved.
ja: 罪悪感に苛まれた Dante は、その墓の足もとで、Cavalcante della Scala が今も vivi の中にいることを quel caduto に伝えてほしいと Farinata に請い、それまでの沈黙は Farinata が今まさに解消した誤解によるものであったと説明した。

## Scene 115-120: Virgil’s List of Souls
en: At *piè de la sua tomba* in *le sepulture*, *Virgilio* summons *Dante*, who first asks *Farinata* who else resides there. *Farinata* replies that he lies among more than a thousand others, mentioning *Federico II* and *il Cardinale* while remaining silent about the other *Eretici*.
ja: 墓地のその墓の足元で、VirgilioはDanteを呼び寄せ、Danteはまず他に誰がここに留まっているのかをFarinataに尋ねた。Farinataは、自分は千を超える者たちと共に横たわっていると答え、Federico IIとil Cardinaleの名を挙げたが、他のEreticiについては沈黙を守った。

## Scene 121-126: Virgil’s Question to Dante
en: At *piè de la sua tomba*, *Farinata* withdraws, and *Dante* turns back toward *Virgilio* while reflecting on the hostile nature of the conversation. *Virgilio* asks why *Dante* seems so bewildered, and *Dante* answers his question.
ja: 墓の足元でFarinataが身を引くと、Danteは先ほどの敵意に満ちた対話に思いを巡らせながら、Virgilioの方へ向き直った。Virgilioがなぜそれほど当惑しているのかと尋ねると、Danteはそれに答えた。

## Scene 127-132: The Command regarding Beatrice
en: At *piè de la sua tomba*, *Virgilio* commands *Dante* to keep in mind what he has heard and wait until he stands before *Beatrice*, from whom he will learn the journey of his life.
ja: その墓前にて、VirgilioはDanteに、これまで聞いたことを心に留めてBeatriceの前に立つまで待つよう命じた。彼女からこそ、彼は自らの人生の旅路について知ることになるのである。

## Scene 133-136: Departure into the Valley
en: *Virgilio* leads the way, and he and *Dante* leave the wall at *piè de la sua tomba* in *le sepulture* to follow a path toward the center and a valley of foul odor.
ja: Virgilioが先導し、彼とDanteはpiè de la sua tomba in le sepultureにある壁を離れ、中心へと続く道と、悪臭の漂う谷へと足を進めた。
