# Canto 10 — The Encounter with Farinata and the Shadows of Dis

## Scene 1-9: Dante’s Inquiry
en: `*Virgilio*` leads `*Dante*` along a `*secreto calle*`, and `*Dante*` asks if the `*li empi*` can be seen.
ja: `Virgilio` は `Dante` を秘密の道へと導き、`Dante` はそこで `li empi` の姿が見えるのかと尋ねた。

## Scene 10-18: The Epicurean’s Reply
en: On the *secreto calle*, an *Eretico* informs *Dante* that the tombs will close when the *Eretici* return to their bodies, noting that this is the resting place of *Epicuro* and *tutti suoi seguaci*.
ja: 秘密の道にて、一人のEreticoがDanteに、Ereticiが肉体に戻るときに墓は閉ざされるのだと告げ、ここはEpicuroとtutti suoi seguaciの安息の地であると語った。

## Scene 19-27: Virgil’s Admonition
en: *Dante* addresses *Virgilio* on the *secreto calle*, and *l'anima epicurea* is mentioned as inviting him to linger.
ja: Danteは秘密の道でVirgilioに語りかけ、そこではl'anima epicureaが彼に留まるよう誘っていた。

## Scene 28-33: The Voice from the Tomb
en: Startled by a voice from a tomb on the *secreto calle*, *Dante* draws near *Virgilio*, who instructs him to turn and behold *Farinata* standing upright from the waist up.
ja: 秘密の小路にある墓から聞こえてきた声に驚いたDanteはVirgilioに歩み寄った。Virgilioは彼に振り返るよう促し、腰から上の身を正して立っているFarinataの姿を彼に見せた。

## Scene 34-39: The Defiance of Farinata
en: *Dante* fixes his gaze on *Farinata*, who rises with a chest and brow of defiance. *Virgilio* pushes *Dante* among *le sepulture* toward *Farinata* and tells him to keep his words brief.
ja: Danteは、不遜に胸を張り、挑むような面持ちで立ち上がるFarinataを凝視した。Virgilioは墓所の中をDanteをFarinataの方へと促し、手短に話すよう告げた。

## Scene 40-48: Farinata’s Inquiry
en: At *piè de la sua tomba*, *Farinata* looks at *Dante* and inquires about *li maggior*; *Dante* answers fully, prompting *Farinata* to recount how he twice dispersed *li maggior*, who were fierce enemies to him and his *primi*.
ja: 墓の足もとで、FarinataはDanteに視線を向け、li maggiorについて尋ねる。Danteが十分に答えると、Farinataは自分とprimiにとって宿敵であったli maggiorをいかに二度追い散らしたかを語り出した。

## Scene 49-51: Dante’s Rebuttal
en: At *piè de la sua tomba*, *Dante* replies to *Farinata* that while the *Guelfi* returned after each exile, the *Ghibellini* never mastered that art.
ja: その墓前で、DanteはFarinataに答えた。Guelfiは追放のたびに帰還したが、Ghibelliniはその術を習得することがなかったのだと。

## Scene 52-60: The Emergence of the Shadow
en: At *piè de la sua tomba*, *un'ombra* rises and looks around *Dante* to see if he is alone. Weeping, *un'ombra* asks *Dante* where *Cavalcante della Scala* is and why he does not accompany him.
ja: 墓のふもとからun'ombraが立ち上がり、Danteが独りであるか確かめるように辺りを見回した。un'ombraは涙ながらに、Cavalcante della Scalaはどこにいるのか、なぜ彼が同行していないのかをDanteに問いかけた。

## Scene 61-66: The Reference to Guido
en: At `*piè de la sua tomba*`, `*Dante*` explains that `*Virgilio*` leads him, referencing `*Guido da Montefeltro*` and the identity of `*il padre di Cavalcante della Scala*`.
ja: 墓の足元で、DanteはVirgilioに導かれていることを語り、Guido da Montefeltroやil padre di Cavalcante della Scalaの正体について言及した。

## Scene 67-72: The Shadow’s Grief
en: At *piè de la sua tomba* in *le sepulture*, *l'ombra* reacts with alarm to the phrasing of *Dante*, fearing that *Cavalcante della Scala* is no longer alive, but then collapses back into the tomb.
ja: 墓地の彼の墓の足元で、l'ombraはDanteの言い回しに驚き、Cavalcante della Scalaがもはや生きていないのではないかと恐れたが、やがて再び墓の中へと崩れ落ちた。

## Scene 73-84: Farinata’s Question on Impiety
en: At `*piè de la sua tomba*` in `*le sepulture*`, `*Farinata*` remains unmoved and tells `*Dante*` that the art of the `*Guelfi*` torments him more than his burial. He predicts that before `*la donna*` is renewed fifty times, `*Dante*` will feel the weight of that art, and asks why the `*Guelfi*` are so impious.
ja: 埋葬地の自らの墓前に立つ`Farinata`は、泰然としたまま、埋葬の苦しみよりも`Guelfi`の策動に苛まれていると`Dante`に語った。彼は、`la donna`が五十度新しくなる前に`Dante`もまたその策の重みを味わうことになると予言し、なぜ`Guelfi`はこれほど不信心なのかと問いかけた。

## Scene 85-87: The Metaphor of the Arbia
en: At *piè de la sua tomba* in *le sepulture*, *Dante* tells *Farinata* that the slaughter which turned a river red is still recounted like a prayer in the temples of his own people.
ja: 墓地に眠る彼の墓の足もとで、DanteはFarinataに、川を赤く染めたあの虐殺が今もなお同胞たちの神殿で祈りのように語り継がれていることを告げた。

## Scene 88-93: Farinata’s Lament for Florence
en: At *piè de la sua tomba* in *le sepulture*, *Farinata* sighs and asserts that although he was not alone in his actions, he was the only one who defended the city openly.
ja: 墓所の己が墓の足元にて、Farinataは嘆息し、自らの行いに同志がなかったわけではないが、公然と都市を守ったのは自分ただ一人であったと断言した。

## Scene 94-99: Dante’s Request for Prophecy
en: At *piè de la sua tomba* in *le sepulture*, *Dante* expresses a wish for the peace of *Farinata*'s *vostra semenza* and begs *Farinata* to resolve a knot of confusion, noting *Farinata*'s ability to see what the future brings and perceive the present differently.
ja: 墓所のその墓の足もとにて、DanteはFarinataのvostra semenzaに平安があることを願い、未来を見通し現在を異なった視点で捉えることができるFarinataの能力に言及しながら、混乱の結び目を解いてほしいと彼に懇願した。

## Scene 100-108: The Limitation of Hell’s Vision
en: At *piè de la sua tomba* in *le sepulture*, *Farinata* explains that *le anime dell'Inferno* can perceive distant events through the light of *il sommo duce*, yet remain blind to the present unless informed by *Dante*. He adds that this knowledge will cease entirely once the door to the future is closed.
ja: sepultureにある自らの墓前にて、Farinataは、le anime dell'Infernoがil sommo duceの光によって遠き出来事を感知できる一方で、Danteに教えられない限り現在は見えないことを語った。そして、未来への扉が閉じれば、こうした知覚は完全に途絶えると付け加えた。

## Scene 109-114: Dante’s Apology
en: At `*piè de la sua tomba*` in `*le sepulture*`, `*Dante*` asks `*Farinata*` to communicate that `*Cavalcante della Scala*` is still among the `*vivi*` and to explain that his previous silence was a result of the confusion `*Farinata*` had just resolved.
ja: 墓所の彼の墓のふもとで、`Dante` は `Farinata` に対し、`Cavalcante della Scala` が今なお `vivi` のなかにいることを伝え、また先ほどの沈黙は `Farinata` が今しがた解消した混乱によるものであったと説明するよう求めた。

## Scene 115-120: Virgil’s List of Souls
en: At *piè de la sua tomba* in *le sepulture*, *Virgilio* calls *Dante* to move on, but *Dante* first asks *Farinata* who else resides there. *Farinata* replies that he lies among more than a thousand *Eretici*, specifically mentioning *Federico II* and *il Cardinale*, but he remains silent about the others.
ja: 墓所の足元にて、VirgilioはDanteに先へ進むよう促すが、Danteはまず他に誰がここに留まっているのかをFarinataに問いかけた。Farinataは、自身が千人以上のEreticiと共に眠っていると答え、Federico IIとil Cardinaleの名を挙げたものの、それ以外の者については沈黙を守った。

## Scene 121-126: Virgil’s Question to Dante
en: At *piè de la sua tomba* in *le sepulture*, *Farinata* disappears, and *Dante* turns to *Virgilio*, unsettled by the hostility of their conversation. *Virgilio* asks *Dante* why he seems so bewildered, and *Dante* answers him.
ja: 墓所の彼の墓の足元でFarinataが姿を消すと、二人の会話に漂う敵意に動揺したDanteはVirgilioの方を向いた。Virgilioが、なぜそれほど当惑しているのかと問いかけると、Danteはそれに答えた。

## Scene 127-132: The Command regarding Beatrice
en: At *piè de la sua tomba* in *le sepulture*, *Virgilio* commands *Dante* to remember what he has heard and to wait until he stands before the light of *Beatrice*, from whom he will learn the journey of his life.
ja: 墓所の自らの墓の足もとで、VirgilioはDanteに、これまで聞いたことを忘れず、Beatriceの光の前に立つ時まで待つよう命じた。その光からこそ、Danteは自らの人生の旅路を悟ることになるのである。

## Scene 133-136: Departure into the Valley
en: At *piè de la sua tomba* in *le sepulture*, *Virgilio* signals the move, and he and *Dante* leave the wall to follow a path toward a valley of unpleasant scent.
ja: 埋葬地の彼の墓の足もとで、Virgilioが移動の合図を送ると、彼とDanteは壁を離れ、不快な臭いの漂う谷へと続く道を辿った。
