# Canto 02 — The Divine Intervention and Dante's Rebirth of Will

## Scene 1-9: Prologue and Invocation
en: As day ends *là dove 'l sol tace*, *Dante* prepares himself for the hardships and the pity of the path ahead, calling upon the *muse* to assist in recording his journey.
ja: 日は暮れ、là dove 'l sol taceの刻。Danteは、これから辿る道の苦難と哀切に備え、自らの旅路を記録する助けを求めてmuseに呼びかけた。

## Scene 10-27: Virgil's Illustrious Lineage
en: In *là dove 'l sol tace*, *Dante* asks *Virgilio* to judge if he is worthy, reflecting on how *il Padre* chose *Enea* to establish a destiny for *li discendenti di Enea* and a place for *il successor del maggior Piero*—a divine purpose apparent to any *omo d'intelletto*.
ja: 太陽の沈黙する地にて、Danteは自らがふさわしい存在であるかをVirgilioに問い、il PadreがいかにしてEneaを選び、li discendenti di Eneaに宿命を、そしてil successor del maggior Pieroに居場所を定めたかという、あらゆるomo d'intellettoにとって明白な神聖なる目的について思いを巡らせた。

## Scene 28-36: Dante's Self-Doubt
en: At *là dove 'l sol tace*, *Dante* doubts his worthiness in comparison to the mentioned *Enëa* and *Paulo*, wondering why *Dio* would grant him such a journey. He admits to *Virgilio* that he is not reasoning rationally and fears that proceeding would be madness.
ja: là dove 'l sol taceにて、Danteは先に出たEnëaやPauloに比して自らの分不相応さを疑い、なぜDioが自分にこのような旅を授けたのかと自問する。彼は理性を失っていることをVirgilioに認め、このまま進むことは狂気であると恐れた。

## Scene 37-42: The Paralysis of Will
en: On the *oscura costa* in *là dove 'l sol tace*, *Dante* compares himself to *quei che disvuol ciò che volle*, a person who erases their own original intention through new doubts. *Dante* confesses that such hesitation has consumed the resolve he felt at the start of his journey.
ja: 太陽が沈黙する彼方の暗き海岸にて、Danteは自らをquei che disvuol ciò che volle、すなわち新たな疑念によって自らの当初の意図を消し去る者に例えた。そのような迷いが、旅の始まりに抱いた決意を蝕んでしまったことを、Danteは告白する。

## Scene 43-51: Virgil's Diagnosis
en: On the *oscura costa* in *là dove 'l sol tace*, `*Virgilio*` tells `*Dante*` that his spirit is hampered by cowardice, a failing that often obstructs `*l'omo*`. To restore his courage, `*Virgilio*` promises to reveal the purpose of his coming and the message he received concerning `*Dante*`.
ja: 太陽が沈黙する地の暗き海岸にて、VirgilioはDanteの精神が臆病さに囚われていることを指摘し、それは l'omo をしばしば阻む弱さであると説いた。彼の勇気を取り戻させるため、Virgilioは自らがここへ来た目的と、Danteについて託された伝言を明かすことを約束した。

## Scene 52-57: The Setting of the Encounter
en: On the *oscura costa* in *là dove 'l sol tace*, *Virgilio* recounts how *Beatrice* called to him among *color che son sospesi*, her eyes shining with a radiance that prompted him to ask for her commands as she spoke in an angelic voice.
ja: 太陽が沈み静まり返った暗き海岸にて、Virgilioは、color che son sospesiの中にいたBeatriceが自分を呼んだ出来事を語る。天使のような声で語りかける彼女の瞳は眩い光を湛えており、彼はその輝きに促されるまま、彼女の命を請うた。

## Scene 58-74: Beatrice's Message
en: On the *oscura costa*, *Beatrice* urges *Virgilio* to assist *Dante*, prompted by *quel ch'i' ho di lui nel cielo udito*, and promises to praise *Virgilio* before *il segnor mio*.
ja: 暗き岸辺にて、Beatriceはquel ch'i' ho di lui nel cielo uditoに促され、VirgilioにDanteを助けるよう請い、il segnor mioの前で彼を称えることを約束した。

## Scene 75-84: Dante's Inquiry
en: At *questo centro de l'ampio loco*, *Dante* expresses his eager readiness to obey the mentioned *Beatrice* and asks *Virgilio* why he does not fear descending into this place.
ja: この広大な場所の中心にて、Danteは前述のBeatriceに従おうとする熱烈な意志を示し、Virgilioに、なぜこの地へ降りることを恐れないのかと問いかけた。

## Scene 85-99: Beatrice's Immunity
en: At *questo centro de l'ampio loco*, *Virgilio* explains to *Dante* that he does not fear the depths because *Beatrice*, created by *Dio*, is immune to its miseries. He reveals that *Beatrice*, moved by compassion for *Dante*, spoke to *Lucia* to facilitate *Virgilio*'s mission.
ja: この広き場所の中心にて、VirgilioはDanteに、Dioに創られたBeatriceはその悲惨さに侵されることがないため、自分は深淵を恐れていないのだと説いた。そして、Danteへの慈しみに突き動かされたBeatriceがLuciaに語りかけ、Virgilioの使命を助けたことを明かした。

## Scene 100-114: Lucia's Intervention
en: At *questo centro de l'ampio loco* in *là dove 'l sol tace*, *Lucia* approaches *Rachele* and *Beatrice*, urging *Beatrice* to save *Dante*, who had distanced himself from *la volgare schiera*. The mentioned *Virgilio* is said to have acted more promptly than any *persone ratte*.
ja: *là dove 'l sol tace* の *questo centro de l'ampio loco* にて、Lucia は Rachele と Beatrice に近づき、la volgare schiera から離れた Dante を救うよう Beatrice に促した。また、前述の Virgilio は、いかなる persone ratte よりも迅速に行動したと言われている。

## Scene 115-117: The Emotional Impact
en: At *questo centro de l'ampio loco* in *là dove 'l sol tace*, *Virgilio* finishes speaking and turns his shining, tearful eyes toward *Dante*, prompting *Dante* to proceed more promptly.
ja: 太陽の届かぬ広大な地の中心にて、Virgilioは語り終えると、涙に濡れて輝く瞳をDanteに向け、速やかに進むよう彼を促した。

## Scene 118-126: Virgil's Final Challenge
en: At *questo centro de l'ampio loco* in *là dove 'l sol tace*, `*Virgilio*` reminds `*Dante*` that he came as `*Beatrice*` willed and has already once rescued him. He rebukes `*Dante*` for his cowardice, urging him to be bold since `*Beatrice*` and `*Lucia*` care for him from heaven.
ja: *questo centro de l'ampio loco*、すなわち *là dove 'l sol tace* において、Virgilioは、Beatriceの意向でここへ来たこと、そして既に一度彼を救い出したことをDanteに思い出させた。彼はDanteの臆病さを叱り、天上のBeatriceとLuciaが彼を案じているのだから、勇気を出すよう促した。

## Scene 127-132: The Metaphor of the Flowers
en: At *questo centro de l'ampio loco* in *là dove 'l sol tace*, *Dante* likens his renewed courage to the way mentioned *fioretti* open when warmed by the sun, feeling a surge of boldness that makes him resolute.
ja: questo centro de l'ampio loco in là dove 'l sol taceにおいて、Danteは自らのうちに蘇った勇気を、陽光に温められて開花するfiorettiになぞらえ、心に湧き上がる大胆な衝動とともに、確固たる決意を抱いた。

## Scene 133-140: Dante's Renewed Commitment
en: At *questo centro de l'ampio loco* in *là dove 'l sol tace*, *Dante* thanks the mentioned *Beatrice* for her mercy and *Virgilio* for his prompt assistance. With his resolve restored, *Dante* commits himself to *Virgilio* as his guide, lord, and master.
ja: 太陽が沈黙する地、その広き場所の中心において、*Dante*は*Beatrice*の慈悲と*Virgilio*の迅速な助けに感謝を捧げた。決意を新たにした*Dante*は、導き手にして主、そして師として、自らを*Virgilio*に委ねた。

## Scene 141-142: The Resumed Journey
en: After *Dante* spoke these words at *questo centro de l'ampio loco* in *là dove 'l sol tace*, *Virgilio* began to move and entered the high, wooded path.
ja: Danteが、太陽の届かぬ広大な場所のこの中心でこれらの言葉を口にすると、Virgilioは歩き出し、高く木々の生い茂る道へと入っていった。
