# Canto 26 — The Eighth Bolgia and the Tale of Ulysses

## Scene 1-12: The Prologue to Florence
en: In *l'ottava ripa*, *Dante* expresses his shame that five *tuoi cittadini* are found among *li ladron* and warns of a future calamity.
ja: l'ottava ripaにて、Danteはli ladronの中に5人のtuoi cittadiniが混じっていることに恥じ入り、将来の災厄を警告した。

## Scene 13-24: The Ascent and Reflection
en: *Dante* and *Virgilio* climb back up *le scalee* in *l'ottava ripa*, struggling through the jagged rocks. Recalling the pain of what he has seen, *Dante* vows to restrain his poetic imagination so that it remains guided by virtue.
ja: DanteとVirgilioは、険しい岩場にもがきながら、第八の岸にある階段を登り返した。目にした光景の痛みを思い返し、Danteは自らの詩的想像力が常に徳に導かれるよう、それを抑制することを誓った。

## Scene 25-33: The Sight of the Eighth Bolgia
en: *Dante*, while on *le scalee*, sees the eighth bolgia glowing with countless flames, comparing the sight to how *'l villan* might perceive fireflies in a valley.
ja: Danteは階段から、無数の炎に照らされて光り輝く第8のボッリジャを目にし、その光景を'l villanが谷間に舞う蛍を眺めるさまに例えた。

## Scene 34-42: The Motion of the Flames
en: Like the story of *Eliseo* watching *Elia* ascend in fire, the flames in the *fosso* swoop through the ditch to envelop and hide *il peccatore* from view.
ja: EliseoがEliaの火の中への昇天を見届けた物語のごとく、溝を駆け抜けた炎がil peccatoreを包み込み、その姿を視界から消し去った。

## Scene 43-48: Virgil's Explanation
en: On *'l ponte*, *Dante* is so mesmerized by the sight that he nearly falls in, prompting *Virgilio* to explain that *li spirti* are wrapped within those flames.
ja: 橋の上で、Danteはその光景に心を奪われて危うく転落しそうになり、それを受けてVirgilioは、その炎の中にli spirtiが包まれているのだと説明した。

## Scene 49-63: The Inquiry of the Trojan Heroes
en: On *'l ponte*, *Dante* asks *Virgilio* who is engulfed in the fire, comparing the sight to the pyre of *Eteòcle* and *Polinice*. *Virgilio* identifies the souls as *Ulisse* and *Dïomede*, citing their fraud that enabled *il gentil seme* to emerge and their deceits involving *Deïdamìa* and *Achille*.
ja: 'l ponteにて、Danteは炎に包まれる者の正体をEteòcleとPoliniceの火葬の薪に例えてVirgilioに問うた。Virgilioは、il gentil semeを出現させた彼らの策略や、DeïdamìaとAchilleを巡る欺瞞を挙げ、その魂がUlisseとDïomedeであることを明かした。

## Scene 64-75: Dante's Request and Virgil's Warning
en: On *'l ponte*, *Dante* intensely pleads with *Virgilio* to wait for the flame's approach so he may speak with the spirits. *Virgilio* agrees but commands *Dante* to remain silent, warning that *i Greci*—including *Ulisse*—might be reluctant to answer him.
ja: 橋の上で、Danteは霊たちと語らうため、炎が近づくのを待ってほしいとVirgilioに強く請い願った。Virgilioは承諾したものの、Ulisseを含むi Greciが答えるのを渋るかもしれないと警告し、Danteに沈黙を守るよう命じた。

## Scene 76-84: The Voice from the Flame
en: On *'l ponte*, *Virgilio* addresses *Ulisse* and the other spirit in the flame, identifying himself as a poet of high verses. He asks which of them was responsible for the ruin that led to his death.
ja: 'l ponteにて、VirgilioはUlisseと、ともに炎に包まれたもう一人の霊に語りかけ、自らを気高い詩を詠む詩人であると名乗った。そして、彼らのどちらが、彼を死に追いやった破滅を招いたのかと問いかけた。

## Scene 85-99: Ulysses's Wandering Impulse
en: On *'l ponte*, *Ulisse* begins to speak, recounting how his passion for exploring the world's virtues and vices overcame his love for his *figlio*, his *vecchio padre*, and *Penelopè*, and how he departed from *Circe*, who had been named by *Enëa*.
ja: 'l ponteにて、Ulisseは語り始めた。世界の美徳と悪徳を探索せんとする情熱が、figlio、vecchio padre、そしてPenelopèへの愛をいかにして凌駕し、Enëaが名を挙げたCirceのもとをいかにして去ったかを。

## Scene 100-105: The Voyage Through the Mediterranean
en: On *'l ponte*, *Ulisse* describes sailing the open sea in a single small ship, reaching distant shores and various islands.
ja: 橋の上で、Ulisseは一隻の小舟で大海原を航海し、遥か遠き岸辺や数多の島々に辿り着いたことを語る。

## Scene 106-120: The Pillars of Hercules and the Great Exhortation
en: On *'l ponte*, *Ulisse* recounts reaching the limit marked by *Ercule* for *l'uom* and leaving *Sibilia* and *Setta*. He then exhorts his *compagni* to venture beyond that boundary, arguing that they were made not to live as brutes but to pursue virtue and knowledge.
ja: 橋の上でUlisseは、Erculeがl'uomのために定めた限界に達し、SibiliaとSettaを後にしたことを語った。そして、人は獣のように生きるためではなく徳と知を追求するために創られたのだと説き、compagniにその境界を越えて進むよう促した。

## Scene 121-126: The Flight of the Crew
en: On `*'l ponte*`, `*Ulisse*` recounts how his speech left `*Li miei compagni*` so eager that they set out on a foolish flight, using their oars as wings.
ja: 橋の上で、Ulisseは、自らの言葉に突き動かされたLi miei compagniが、櫂を翼にして愚かな飛翔へと打って出たことを語る。

## Scene 127-132: Descent into the Southern Seas
en: On `*'l ponte*`, `*Ulisse*` describes seeing the stars of the opposite pole while the familiar one sank below the horizon, noting that the moon had waxed and waned five times since he and `*i suoi compagni*` had entered the high pass.
ja: 甲板に立ち、`Ulisse` は、見慣れた極星が地平線の下へと沈み、代わりに反対側の極の星々が見えるようになったことを語った。彼と `i suoi compagni` があの高い峠を越えてから、月は五度満ち欠けしたという。

## Scene 133-142: The Final Storm and Shipwreck
en: On *'l ponte*, *Ulisse* recounts how he and *i suoi compagni* sighted a towering mountain, but their joy turned to grief when a whirlwind struck the ship, spinning it three times before pulling them under the waves.
ja: 橋の上で、Ulisseは、自身とi suoi compagniがそびえ立つ山を目にしたものの、つむじ風が船を三度回転させて波の下へと引きずり込み、その喜びが悲しみへと変わったことを語った。
