# Canto 16 — The Encounter with the Three Florentine Souls and the Approaching Figure

## Scene 1-12: The Appearance of the Wounded Souls
en: While on *la strada*, *Dante* and *Virgilio* are approached by *tre ombre* who have separated themselves from *una torma*. Recognizing *Dante*'s clothing, *tre ombre* speak to him, and *Dante* is deeply moved by the sight of their fire-scorched wounds.
ja: 道を歩んでいたDanteとVirgilioのもとに、una tormaから離れたtre ombreが近づいてきた。Danteの服装に気づいたtre ombreが彼に話しかけると、Danteはその火に焼かれた傷跡を目の当たりにし、深く心を揺さぶられた。

## Scene 13-18: Virgil's Instruction
en: *Virgilio* listens to the cries of *i fiorentini* and tells *Dante* to wait and be courteous to them. He remarks that, were it not for the rain of fire on *la strada*, *Dante* should be in more of a hurry than *i fiorentini*.
ja: Virgilioはi fiorentiniの叫びに耳を傾け、Danteに待って礼儀正しく接するように告げた。道に降る火の雨さえなければ、i fiorentiniよりもむしろDanteこそが急ぐべきである、と彼は述べた。

## Scene 19-27: The Rotation of the Three
en: *Virgilio* and *Dante* resume their way on *la strada*, where the *tre fiorentini* join them and circle around them like *i campion*, each keeping their eyes fixed on *Dante*.
ja: VirgilioとDanteが再び道を進むと、tre fiorentiniが合流し、i campionのように彼らの周囲を巡りながら、それぞれがDanteに視線を据えていた。

## Scene 28-45: The Identification of the Souls
en: On *la strada*, *Iacopo Rusticucci* asks *Dante* to identify himself and introduces his companions, *Guido Guerra*—a grandson of *Gualdrada*—and *Tegghiaio Aldobrandi*. *Iacopo Rusticucci* then names himself, lamenting that *la fiera moglie* causes him more harm than anything else.
ja: 道すがら、Iacopo RusticucciはDanteに名乗るよう求め、連れのGuido Guerra――Gualdradaの孫である――とTegghiaio Aldobrandiを紹介した。そしてIacopo Rusticucciは自らも名乗り、la fiera moglieが何よりも自分に害をもたらしていると嘆いた。

## Scene 46-51: Dante's Hesitation
en: On *la strada*, *Dante* longs to embrace *Guido Guerra*, *Tegghiaio Aldobrandi*, and *Iacopo Rusticucci*, but the fear of being burned outweighs his desire. He reflects that *Virgilio* would have permitted such an encounter had he already been subject to the fire.
ja: la stradaにおいて、DanteはGuido Guerra、Tegghiaio Aldobrandi、そしてIacopo Rusticucciを抱きしめたいと切望したが、火に焼かれることへの恐怖がその想いに勝った。もし自分がすでに火に焼かれていたならば、Virgilioもこのような邂逅を許してくれただろうと彼は考える。

## Scene 52-63: Dante's Response
en: On *la strada*, *Dante* tells *Guido Guerra*, *Tegghiaio Aldobrandi*, and *Iacopo Rusticucci* that his silence was caused by pity for their condition and his admiration for their names. He explains that although *Virgilio* has promised him sweet fruits, he must first descend to the center.
ja: la stradaにて、DanteはGuido Guerra、Tegghiaio Aldobrandi、そしてIacopo Rusticucciに、自らが沈黙していたのは彼らの境遇への哀れみと、その名への敬愛ゆえであったと告げた。また、Virgilioに甘き果実を約束されてはいるが、まずは中心へと降りなくてはならないことを説明した。

## Scene 64-72: The Souls' Inquiry
en: On *la strada*, *Iacopo Rusticucci* asks *Dante* if he is still alive and if courtesy and valor still reside in their city, explaining that the reports of *Guiglielmo Borsiere*—who often returns to his *compagni*—have brought great distress to the *tre anime fiorentine*.
ja: 道の上で、Iacopo RusticucciはDanteに、彼がまだ生きているのか、そして彼らの街に今も礼節と勇気が残っているのかを尋ねた。よくcompagniのもとへ戻るGuiglielmo Borsiereの報告が、tre anime fiorentineに大きな苦しみをもたらしていたためである。

## Scene 73-75: Dante's Political Rebuke
en: On `*la strada*`, the speaker explains that `*La gente nuova*` and sudden gains have bred pride and excess in the city, which is told it should already be weeping.
ja: 道すがら語り手は、`La gente nuova`と不意の富が街に慢心と贅沢をもたらしたと説く。その街は、いまや涙を流すべき時にあるという。

## Scene 76-85: The Request for Remembrance
en: On *la strada*, *Guido Guerra*, *Tegghiaio Aldobrandi*, and *Iacopo Rusticucci* look at one another after *Dante*'s frank response and, praising his honesty, urge him to speak of them to the *gente* should he return to the world above.
ja: la stradaにおいて、Danteの率直な答えを受けたGuido Guerra、Tegghiaio Aldobrandi、そしてIacopo Rusticucciは互いに顔を見合わせ、その誠実さを称えながら、もし上の世界に戻ることがあればgenteに自分たちのことを伝えてほしいと彼に請うた。

## Scene 86-90: The Departure of the Souls
en: On *la strada*, *Guido Guerra*, *Tegghiaio Aldobrandi*, and *Iacopo Rusticucci* suddenly break their circle and vanish so quickly that no final word can be exchanged with *Dante*, leading *Virgilio* to decide that it is time for them to depart.
ja: la stradaにて、Guido Guerra、Tegghiaio Aldobrandi、そして Iacopo Rusticucciが突如としてその輪を破り、Danteが最後の一言を交わす間もなく瞬く間に消え去った。これを受けてVirgilioは、いまこそ出発の時であると判断した。

## Scene 91-105: The Sound of the Water
en: On *la strada*, *Dante* and *Virgilio* are followed by a roar of water so loud that it nearly drowns out their speech. They eventually reach a steep slope where they encounter the sound of blood-tinted water thundering below.
ja: 道を行くDanteとVirgilioの背後から、二人の話し声さえかき消さんばかりに激しい水の轟音が迫る。やがて彼らが辿り着いた急斜面の下からは、血に染まった水が猛烈に唸る音が響き渡っていた。

## Scene 106-114: The Use of the Rope
en: On the *sponda*, *Dante* unties the rope around his waist—which he had once thought of using to capture a beast—and hands it to *Virgilio*, who throws it down into the ravine.
ja: 岸辺で Dante は、かつて獣を捕らえるために使おうと考えていた腰の縄を解いて Virgilio に手渡した。Virgilio はそれを、谷底へと投げ捨てた。

## Scene 115-120: Dante's Reflection on Prudence
en: On the *sponda*, *Dante* tells himself he must respond to a new sign from *Virgilio* and reflects on how cautious *li uomini* should be when facing *color che non veggion pur l'ovra*.
ja: 岸辺にて、DanteはVirgilioからの新たな合図に応じねばならないと自らに言い聞かせ、color che non veggion pur l'ovraと対峙する際、li uominiがいかに慎重であるべきかと思索した。

## Scene 121-126: Virgil's Warning
en: On the *sponda*, *Virgilio* tells *Dante* that what he awaits will soon be revealed, warning that `*l'uom*` should remain silent when the truth appears to be a lie to avoid undue shame.
ja: 岸辺にてVirgilioはDanteに、待ち望んでいたものは間もなく明らかになると告げた。そして真実が嘘に見えるときは、不必要な恥を避けるためl'uomは沈黙を守るべきだと警告した。

## Scene 127-136: The Approaching Figure
en: On the *sponda*, *Dante* swears to the *lettor* that he witnessed *Gerione* swimming upward through the thick air, moving like *colui che va giuso* as he ascends from the sea.
ja: 岸辺に立つDanteは、Gerioneが濃い空気の中を上方へ泳ぎ上がるさまを、まるで海から浮上するcolui che va giusoのように動いていたとlettorに誓う。
