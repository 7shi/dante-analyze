# Canto 16 — The Encounter with the Three Florentine Souls and the Approaching Figure

## Scene 1-12: The Appearance of the Wounded Souls
en: While traveling along *la strada*, *Dante* and *Virgilio* are approached by *tre ombre* who break away from *una torma*. Recognizing *Dante* by his clothing, the *tre ombre* call out to him, leaving *Dante* pained by the sight of their fire-seared wounds.
ja: 道をゆくDanteとVirgilioのもとに、una tormaから離れたtre ombreが近づいてきた。その装束からDanteであることに気づいたtre ombreが彼に呼びかけるが、火に焼かれた傷跡を目の当たりにしたDanteは、深い痛みに襲われた。

## Scene 13-18: Virgil's Instruction
en: *Virgilio* tells *Dante* to wait and be courteous to *i fiorentini*, noting that if it were not for the raining fire on *la strada*, *Dante* should be the one in more of a hurry.
ja: VirgilioはDanteに、待ってi fiorentiniに礼儀正しく接するように諭した。道に火が降り注いでさえいなければ、むしろ急ぐべきはDanteの方なのだ、と。

## Scene 19-27: The Rotation of the Three
en: *Virgilio* resumes his way on *la strada*, and as the *tre fiorentini* reach them, they circle *Dante* and *Virgilio* like *i campion* are said to do, each keeping its gaze fixed on *Dante*.
ja: Virgilioが再び道を歩み出すと、追いついたtre fiorentiniは、i campionがそうすると言われるようにDanteとVirgilioの周囲を巡り、それぞれがDanteをじっと見据えた。

## Scene 28-45: The Identification of the Souls
en: On *la strada*, *Iacopo Rusticucci* asks *Dante* for his identity and introduces his companions: *Guido Guerra*, grandson of *Gualdrada*, and *Tegghiaio Aldobrandi*. *Iacopo Rusticucci* then identifies himself, lamenting that *la fiera moglie* causes him more harm than any other.
ja: 道の上で、Iacopo RusticucciはDanteに正体を問い、同行者であるGualdradaの孫のGuido GuerraとTegghiaio Aldobrandiを紹介した。続いてIacopo Rusticucciは自らの正体を明かし、la fiera moglieが誰よりも自分を苦しめていると嘆いた。

## Scene 46-51: Dante's Hesitation
en: *Dante* longs to embrace *Guido Guerra*, *Tegghiaio Aldobrandi*, and *Iacopo Rusticucci* on *la strada*, but fear of the fire overcomes his will. He believes *Virgilio* would have permitted such an embrace had he already been cast down among them.
ja: Danteは la stradaにて Guido Guerra, Tegghiaio Aldobrandi, そして Iacopo Rusticucci を抱きしめたいと切望したが、炎への恐怖がその意志を屈服させた。もし自分がすでに彼らと共に投げ落とされていたならば、Virgilioもそのような抱擁を許してくれただろうと彼は考えた。

## Scene 52-63: Dante's Response
en: On *la strada*, *Dante* tells *Guido Guerra*, *Tegghiaio Aldobrandi*, and *Iacopo Rusticucci* that his hesitation was born of pity for their condition, not disdain. He expresses his lifelong admiration for them and explains that although *Virgilio* has promised him sweetness, he must first descend to the very center.
ja: 道の上で、DanteはGuido Guerra、Tegghiaio Aldobrandi、そしてIacopo Rusticucciに、自らのためらいは軽蔑ではなく彼らの境遇への憐れみから生じたものであると告げた。彼は彼らへの終生の敬慕を伝え、Virgilioに甘美なる約束をされたとはいえ、まずは最深部へと降りなくてはならないことを語った。

## Scene 64-72: The Souls' Inquiry
en: On *la strada*, *Iacopo Rusticucci* asks *Dante* if he is still alive and if their city still possesses courtesy and valor, explaining that the reports of *Guiglielmo Borsiere*, who associates with his *compagni*, have distressed the *tre anime fiorentine*.
ja: 道の上で、Iacopo RusticucciはDanteに、彼がまだ生きているのか、そして彼らの街に今も礼節と勇気が残っているのかを問いかけた。compagniと共に過ごすGuiglielmo Borsiereの知らせが、tre anime fiorentineを深く憂慮させているのだと説明しながらのことだった。

## Scene 73-75: Dante's Political Rebuke
en: On *la strada*, he declares that *La gente nuova* and sudden wealth have bred pride and excess, leaving the city to weep.
ja: 通りにて、彼は La gente nuova と突然の富が傲慢と贅沢をはびこらせ、街を嘆き悲しませていると断じた。

## Scene 76-85: The Request for Remembrance
en: On *la strada*, after *Dante* speaks his mind, *one of the three Florentine souls* and *another of the three Florentine souls* exchange glances. *Guido Guerra*, *Tegghiaio Aldobrandi*, and *Iacopo Rusticucci* then urge *Dante*, if he should return to the stars, to speak of them to the *gente*.
ja: Danteが本心を語った後、道の上でone of the three Florentine soulsとanother of the three Florentine soulsが視線を交わした。そしてGuido Guerra、Tegghiaio Aldobrandi、Iacopo Rusticucciの三人は、もしDanteが星々へと帰るならば、自分たちのことをgenteに伝えてほしいと彼に請うた。

## Scene 86-90: The Departure of the Souls
en: On *la strada*, *Guido Guerra*, *Tegghiaio Aldobrandi*, and *Iacopo Rusticucci* suddenly break their circle and flee so rapidly that they vanish before any final word can be spoken to *Dante*, prompting *Virgilio* to decide it is time to move on.
ja: 道の上で、Guido Guerra, Tegghiaio Aldobrandi, そして Iacopo Rusticucci は突如としてその輪をほどき、Danteに最後の一言をかける間もなく急速に逃げ去って姿を消した。それを見た Virgilio は、もう先へ進む時だと判断した。

## Scene 91-105: The Sound of the Water
en: *Dante* follows *Virgilio* along *la strada*, but the roar of nearby water soon becomes so loud that they can barely hear one another. They reach a steep drop where the sound of the tinted water thunders with an intensity that threatens to deafen them.
ja: Danteは道を辿りVirgilioの後をついていくが、やがて近くの水の轟音が激しくなり、互いの声がほとんど聞こえなくなる。彼らが辿り着いた切り立った崖では、色づいた水の音が耳をつんざくほどの激しさで轟いていた。

## Scene 106-114: The Use of the Rope
en: On the *sponda*, *Dante* unties a rope he had been carrying and hands it to *Virgilio*, who throws it down into the depths.
ja: 岸辺でDanteは携えていた紐をほどいてVirgilioに渡し、Virgilioはそれを深淵へと投げ入れた。

## Scene 115-120: Dante's Reflection on Prudence
en: On the *sponda*, *Dante* tells himself he must respond to the new sign *Virgilio* signals with his eye. He reflects that *Li uomini* should be cautious around *color che non veggion pur l'ovra*, who perceive a person's inner thoughts rather than just their outward deeds.
ja: 岸辺で、DanteはVirgilioが視線で送った新たな合図に応じねばならないと自らに言い聞かせた。そして、外面的な行いではなく人の内心を見通すcolor che non veggion pur l'ovraに対し、Li uominiは慎重であるべきだと彼は考えた。

## Scene 121-126: Virgil's Warning
en: On the *sponda*, *Virgilio* tells *Dante* that what he expects and *Dante* anticipates will soon be revealed, warning that *l'uom* should remain silent when truth looks like a lie to avoid shame.
ja: 岸辺にて、VirgilioはDanteに、彼が期待しDanteが予期していることが間もなく明らかになると告げ、恥をさらさぬよう、真実が嘘のように見えるときこそl'uomは沈黙を守るべきだと諭した。

## Scene 127-136: The Approaching Figure
en: On the *sponda*, *Dante* swears to the *lettor* that he witnessed *Gerione* swimming upward through the thick, dark air like a diver returning to the surface.
ja: 岸辺に立つDanteは、Gerioneが水面へと戻る潜水士のように、濃く暗い空気の中を上方へと泳ぎ上がるのを目の当たりにしたとlettorに誓っている。
