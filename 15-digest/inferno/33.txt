# Canto 33 — The Tale of Count Ugolino and the Frozen Souls

## Scene 1-9: The Sinner’s Pause
en: At the *lago*, a soul pauses from gnawing *al traditor* to tell *Dante* that he will speak only if his words bring further infamy to the victim.
ja: 湖にて、ある魂がal traditorを齧る手を止め、Danteに語りかけた。自分の言葉が犠牲者にさらなる不名誉をもたらすのであれば、話してやろう、と。

## Scene 10-15: The Introduction
en: At the *lago*, *conte Ugolino* recognizes *Dante*'s voice and identifies himself and *arcivescovo Ruggieri*, promising to explain why they are so close.
ja: 湖のほとりで、conte UgolinoはDanteの声に気づいて自らとarcivescovo Ruggieriの正体を明かし、なぜ二人がこのように寄り添っているのかを説明すると約束した。

## Scene 16-27: The Imprisonment
en: At the *lago*, *conte Ugolino* explains to *Dante* that his trust in *arcivescovo Ruggieri* led to his capture and a cruel death. He describes months of imprisonment—a place where *altrui* might also be shut—until a nightmare revealed his future.
ja: 湖のほとりで、conte UgolinoはDanteに、arcivescovo Ruggieriを信じたことが自身の捕縛と残酷な死を招いたのだと語る。そして、ある悪夢が未来を告げるまで、altruiさえも閉じ込められる場所で過ごした数ヶ月の幽閉生活について詳述した。

## Scene 28-36: The Prison Landscape
en: At the *lago*, *conte Ugolino* describes a vision in which *arcivescovo Ruggieri* hunts him and *i figli* using hounds associated with the names of *Gualandi*, *Sismondi*, and *Lanfranchi*. While *i Pisan* are mentioned in the distance, the pack tears into the exhausted father and *i figli*.
ja: 湖にて、conte Ugolinoは、arcivescovo RuggieriがGualandi、Sismondi、そしてLanfranchiの名を冠した猟犬を使い、自身とi figliを狩るという幻視を語る。遠方でi Pisanの名が口にされるなか、猟犬の群れは疲れ果てた父とi figliに激しく襲いかかった。

## Scene 37-54: The Morning of Hunger
en: At the *lago*, *conte Ugolino* tells *Dante* of waking to *i figliuoli di Ugolino* weeping for bread and asks if *Dante* feels pity for them. Despite the concern of *Anselmuccio*, *conte Ugolino* maintains a heavy silence for the following day and night.
ja: 湖のほとりにて、conte Ugolinoはi figliuoli di Ugolinoがパンを求めて泣き叫ぶなかで目覚めたことをDanteに語り、彼らへの憐れみを感じるかと問いかけた。Anselmuccioの懸念にもかかわらず、conte Ugolinoはその後、一日一夜にわたって重苦しい沈黙を守り続けた。

## Scene 55-66: The Terrible Choice
en: At the *lago*, *conte Ugolino* describes biting his hands in grief, prompting *Anselmuccio* to offer his own flesh for food. To avoid further distress, *conte Ugolino* remains silent for two days, wishing the earth had opened to swallow them.
ja: 湖のほとりで、conte Ugolinoは悲嘆のあまり自らの手をかじったことを語り、それに促されたAnselmuccioは自らの肉を食料として差し出した。さらなる苦痛を避けるため、conte Ugolinoは二日間にわたり沈黙を守り、いっそ大地が割れて自分たちを飲み込んでくれればいいと願った。

## Scene 67-75: The Death of the Children
en: At the *lago*, *conte Ugolino* tells *Dante* that *Gaddo* died on the fourth day, followed by *i figli di Ugolino* by the sixth. Blinded, *conte Ugolino* called to them for two days after their deaths before starvation finally claimed him.
ja: 湖のほとりで、conte Ugolino は Dante に、4日目に Gaddo が死に、6日目までには i figli di Ugolino も後を追ったことを語った。盲目となった conte Ugolino は、彼らの死後も二日間、彼らを呼び続け、ついには飢えに屈して息絶えた。

## Scene 76-78: Resumption of the Feast
en: Having finished his story at the *lago*, *conte Ugolino* turns back with distorted eyes and resumes gnawing the skull with teeth as strong as a dog's.
ja: lagoでの語りを終えたconte Ugolinoは、歪んだ目で振り返ると、犬のように強い歯で再び頭蓋骨をかじり始めた。

## Scene 79-90: The Curse on Pisa
en: At the *lago*, a curse is pronounced upon the *vicini* and *ogne persona*, asserting that *i figliuoi*—including *Uguiccione*, *la brigata*, *Anselmuccio*, and *Gaddo*—did not deserve their fate regardless of the betrayal by *conte Ugolino*.
ja: lagoにて、viciniとogne personaへの呪詛が唱えられた。それは、たとえconte Ugolinoによる裏切りがあったとしても、Uguiccione、la brigata、Anselmuccio、Gaddoを含むi figliuoiが、あのような運命に遭うべきではなかったと断じるものであった。

## Scene 91-108: Entering Cocytus
en: At the *lago*, *Dante* and *Virgilio* pass *un'altra gente* frozen flat whose tears crystallize over their eyes. When *Dante* asks *Virgilio* what causes the wind he feels, *Virgilio* tells him he will soon see the cause, *Lucifero*.
ja: 湖のほとりで、DanteとVirgilioは、涙が目の上で結晶となり平らに凍りついたun'altra genteの傍らを通り過ぎる。Danteが身に感じる風の原因をVirgilioに問うと、その正体であるLuciferoを間もなく目にすることになると彼は答えた。

## Scene 109-117: The Cry for Unmasking
en: At the *fredda crosta*, *un de' tristi* begs *Dante* and *Virgilio* to clear the frozen masks from his eyes so he may weep. *Dante* responds that he will assist *un de' tristi* only if he reveals his identity, otherwise he will move on toward the bottom of the *lago*.
ja: 冷たい氷の殻の上で、un de' tristi が Dante と Virgilio に、涙を流せるよう目の凍った仮面を取り除いてほしいと懇願した。Dante は、un de' tristi が正体を明かすのであれば助けるが、さもなくば湖の底へと先へ進むと答えた。

## Scene 118-126: The Identity of Alberigo
en: In *Tolomea*, *frate Alberigo* reveals his identity to *Dante*, explaining that he now suffers the consequences of his deceits. When *Dante* asks if he is truly dead, *frate Alberigo* notes that *l'anima* often descends to this place before *Atropòs* has cut the thread of life.
ja: Tolomeaにて、frate AlberigoはDanteに自らの正体を明かし、かつての欺瞞ゆえに今この苦しみを味わっているのだと語った。Danteが本当に死んでいるのかと問うと、frate Alberigoは、Atropòsが命の糸を切る前にl'animaがこの地に降り立つことはしばしばあるのだと答えた。

## Scene 127-138: The Mechanics of the Tolomea
en: In *Tolomea*, *Fra Alberigo* explains to *Dante* that as soon as *l'anima* commits betrayal, the soul is cast down and a *demonio* takes over the physical body. He points to *ser Branca Doria* as an example of someone whose soul has been imprisoned in the ice for many years.
ja: Tolomeaにて、Fra AlberigoはDanteに、l'animaが裏切りを犯した途端に魂は突き落とされ、その肉体をdemonioが乗っ取るのだと説いた。そして、長きにわたり氷の中に魂を囚われている例として、ser Branca Doriaを指し示した。

## Scene 139-150: The Dispute with Alberigo
en: In *Tolomea*, *Dante* doubts that *Branca Doria* is dead, but *Alberigo* explains that *Michel Zanche* of the *Malebranche* placed *il diavolo* and *un suo prossimano* in the bodies of the traitors to fake their survival. Despite *Alberigo*'s plea for help, *Dante* refuses to clear the ice from his eyes, deciding that being rude to him is the only true courtesy.
ja: Tolomeaにて、DanteはBranca Doriaが本当に死んでいるのかと疑うが、AlberigoはMalebrancheのMichel Zancheが裏切り者たちの生存を偽装するため、その体にil diavoloとun suo prossimanoを潜り込ませたと説明した。助けを求めるAlberigoの嘆願にもかかわらず、Danteは彼の目から氷を取り除くことを拒み、不親切に接することこそが彼に対する唯一の真の礼節であると考えた。

## Scene 151-157: The Curse on Genoa
en: In *Tolomea*, *Dante* curses the *Genovesi* and wonders why they have not been wiped from the earth, citing *Alberigo* as a man worse than the *peggiore spirto di Romagna*. He observes that *Alberigo*'s soul is already frozen in the ice while his body still appears to live above.
ja: Tolomeaにて、DanteはGenovesiを呪い、Alberigoをpeggiore spirto di Romagnaよりもさらに邪悪な男として挙げ、なぜ彼らが地上から消し去られていないのかと訝しんだ。Alberigoの肉体は地上でなお生きているように見えるが、その魂はすでに氷の中に凍りついているのだと、彼は見抜いていた。
