# Canto 32 — The Frozen Landscape of Cocytus and the Encounters with Traitors

## Scene 1-12: The Poet’s Prologue
en: At the *ripa*, *Dante* acknowledges that describing the bottom of the universe is beyond any tongue born of *la mamma* or *babbo*. He prays that the *muse* who helped *Anfïone* will guide his poetry to ensure his account is accurate.
ja: 岸辺にて、Danteは、宇宙の底を記述することはla mammaやbabboから生まれたいかなる言葉をも超えているのだと悟る。そして、自らの記述が正確なものであるよう、Anfïoneを助けたmuseがその詩を導いてくれることを祈った。

## Scene 13-21: The Descent into the Pit
en: *Dante* addresses the *plebe*, remarking that they would be like frightened sheep in the *pozzo scuro*. Having descended below the feet of *Anteo*, *Dante* is warned by *Virgilio* to watch where he steps so as not to tread upon the *fratei miseri lassi*.
ja: Danteはplebeに、暗き穴の中では彼らも怯えた羊のようになろうと語りかけた。Anteoの足下まで降りたDanteは、fratei miseri lassiを踏まぬよう足元に注意せよとVirgilioに警告された。

## Scene 22-30: The Frozen Lake of Cocytus
en: *Dante* turns to find himself standing above a *lago* of ice so thick and glass-like that even a falling mountain would not crack its edge.
ja: Danteが振り返ると、そこには氷の湖が広がっていた。それはあまりに厚く、ガラスのように滑らかで、たとえ山が崩れ落ちてきたとしても、その端にひび一つ入らぬほどであった。

## Scene 31-39: The Sinners in the Ice
en: In the *ghiaccia*, *l'ombre dolenti* are frozen and face downward, their miserable state likened to a dream of *la villana*.
ja: 氷原に凍りつき、うつむくl'ombre dolenti。その惨めなありさまは、la villanaの見る夢に例えられていた。

## Scene 40-51: The Two Intertwined Sinners
en: In the *ghiaccia*, *Dante* finds two figures frozen so closely that their hair is intertwined. When he asks who they are, their tears freeze, bonding them even more firmly after they had collided in a fit of rage.
ja: 氷の地で、Danteは髪が絡み合うほど密接に凍りついた二人の姿を見つける。正体を問うと、激昂して衝突した彼らの涙が凍りつき、二人はさらに固く結びついた。

## Scene 52-69: The Deaf Sinner’s Revelation
en: In the *ghiaccia*, *Camiscion de' Pazzi* informs *Dante* that *cotesti due* come from *Alberto* and *Bisenzo* and are more deserving of their state than *l'ombre* like *Sassol Mascheroni* or *quelli a cui fu rotto il petto* by *Artù*. He reveals his identity and says he awaits *Carlin*.
ja: 氷原にて、Camiscion de' PazziはDanteに、cotesti dueがAlbertoとBisenzoから来た者であり、Sassol Mascheroniのようなl'ombreやArtùによるquelli a cui fu rotto il pettoよりも、その境遇にふさわしいのだと告げた。彼は自らの正体を明かし、Carlinを待っていると語った。

## Scene 70-78: Walking through the Frozen Multitude
en: In the *ghiaccia*, *Dante* is filled with lasting disgust by the frozen faces he sees. As he and *Virgilio* make their way toward the center of the *lago*, *Dante* trembles from the noise and accidentally steps hard on a face.
ja: 氷原で、Danteは目にする凍りついた顔々に消えぬ嫌悪感を抱いた。Virgilioと共に湖の中心へと向かうなか、Danteは騒音に身を震わせ、誤ってある顔を強く踏みつけてしまった。

## Scene 79-84: The Scolding Sinner
en: Weeping in the *ghiaccia*, *il peccatore* scolds *Dante* for disturbing him. *Dante* asks *Virgilio* to wait a moment while he resolves a doubt concerning *il peccatore*.
ja: 氷の中で涙を流すil peccatoreは、自分を邪魔したDanteを激しく咎めた。Danteはil peccatoreへの疑問を解消するため、Virgilioにしばし待つよう請うた。

## Scene 85-96: The Verbal Dispute
en: While *Virgilio* stands aside in *l'Antenora*, *Dante* questions the frozen soul he has disturbed, but the spirit responds with hostility and rejects *Dante*'s offer of poetic fame.
ja: Virgilioがl'Antenoraで傍らに控えるなか、Danteは自らが揺り起こした氷に閉ざされた魂に問いかけるが、その霊は敵意をもって応じ、詩による名声を授けようというDanteの申し出を拒絶した。

## Scene 97-105: The Physical Altercation
en: In *l'Antenora*, *Dante* seizes *il peccatore* by the scalp, threatening to rip out his hair unless he reveals his identity. Despite this threat, *il peccatore* remains defiant, and *Dante* proceeds to tear away several locks of hair as *il peccatore* barks in response.
ja: その地で、Danteはil peccatoreの頭皮を掴み、正体を明かさなければ髪をむしり取ると脅した。しかしil peccatoreは反抗的な態度を崩さず、Danteが数束の髪をむしり取ると、il peccatoreは吠え返った。

## Scene 106-114: The Interruption by Bocca
en: In *l'Antenora*, *L'altro* mocks *Bocca* and asks what *diavol* is bothering him, revealing the sinner's identity to *Dante*. *Dante* vows to report *Bocca*'s shame, and *Bocca* tells him to go away but to remember *L'altro* if he ever returns to the *lago*.
ja: その地で、L'altroはBoccaを嘲笑い、いかなるdiavolが彼を苛んでいるのかと問うことで、Danteにその罪人の正体を明かした。DanteがBoccaの恥辱を言いふらすと誓うと、Boccaは彼に立ち去れと告げながらも、もし再びこの湖に戻ることがあればL'altroのことを覚えておけと伝えた。

## Scene 115-123: The Catalog of Traitors
en: In *l'Antenora*, *Bocca* tells *Dante* to record that *il peccatore* weeps over the silver of the *Franceschi* and to name *Beccheria* among *i peccatori*. He further suggests that *Gianni de' Soldanier* is frozen deeper in the *lago* with *Ganellone* and *Tebaldello*.
ja: l'Antenoraにて、BoccaはDanteに、il peccatoreがFranceschiの銀を嘆いて泣いていることを記録し、i peccatoriの中にBeccheriaの名を記せと告げた。さらに彼は、Gianni de' SoldanierがGanelloneやTebaldelloと共に、湖のより深い底で凍りついていることを明かした。

## Scene 124-132: The Biting Sinners
en: Having departed from *Bocca*, *Dante* and *Virgilio* encounter *due ghiacciati* in *l'Antenora*, where *'l sovran* is seen biting the other at the base of the skull. *Dante* likens this savage act to how *Tidëo* once tore at the head of *Menalippo*.
ja: Boccaを後にしたDanteとVirgilioは、l'Antenoraにてdue ghiacciatiに遭遇する。そこでは'l sovranがもう一人の後頭部を噛みちぎっており、Danteはその残虐な光景を、かつてTidëoがMenalippoの頭を食い破った様子になぞらえた。

## Scene 133-139: Dante’s Inquiry into Hatred
en: In *l'Antenora*, *Dante* asks *l'ombra che morde* why he exhibits such beastly hatred toward *l'ombra morsa*, offering to preserve their identities and sins in the world above if *le due ombre* explain their history.
ja: l'Antenoraにおいて、Danteは l'ombra che morde に対し、なぜ l'ombra morsa へとこれほど獣のような憎しみを向けるのかを問い、le due ombre がその経緯を明かすのであれば、地上で彼らの正体と罪を後世に伝えようと申し出た。
