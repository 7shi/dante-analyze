# Canto 32 — The Frozen Landscape of Cocytus and the Encounters with Traitors

## Scene 1-12: The Poet’s Prologue
en: At the *ripa*, *Dante* admits that describing this place is beyond anyone born of *la mamma* or *babbo*, and he prays that the *muse* who helped *Anfïone* will aid his verse.
ja: 岸辺にて、Danteはこの地の描写は la mamma や babbo から生まれたいかなる人間にとっても不可能であると認め、かつて Anfïone を助けた muse が自らの詩を導いてくれるよう祈った。

## Scene 13-21: The Descent into the Pit
en: *Dante* addresses the *plebe*, noting their helplessness, as he and *Virgilio* are lowered into the *pozzo scuro* by *Anteo*. As *Dante* looks upward, *Virgilio* warns him to watch his step so that he does not tread upon the heads of the *fratei miseri lassi*.
ja: Anteoによって暗い井戸へと降ろされるなか、Danteはplebeのなすすべもない様子を嘆いて彼らに語りかける。上方を仰ぎ見るDanteに、Virgilioはfratei miseri lassiの頭を踏まぬよう、足元に注意せよと警告した。

## Scene 22-30: The Frozen Lake of Cocytus
en: *Dante* turns and sees a *lago* of ice at his feet that looks more like glass than water. This ice is so thick that even the fall of mountains would not cause it to crack.
ja: Danteが振り返ると、足元には水というよりはむしろガラスのように見える氷の湖が広がっていた。その氷はあまりに厚く、たとえ山が崩れ落ちたとしてもひび一つ入らぬほどであった。

## Scene 31-39: The Sinners in the Ice
en: Within the *ghiaccia*, *l'ombre dolenti* are frozen and livid, like those dreaming of being *la villana*. Their faces are turned downward, and the cold entering their mouths and the sorrow in their eyes testify to their misery.
ja: 氷原の中で、l'ombre dolentiは凍てつき、蒼白に染まっている。その様はあたかもla villanaになることを夢見る者のようで、うつむいた顔、口に忍び込む冷気、そして瞳に宿る悲しみが、彼らの惨めさを物語っている。

## Scene 40-51: The Two Intertwined Sinners
en: On the *ghiaccia*, *Dante* questions two figures frozen so tightly that their hair is intertwined, watching as their tears freeze and bind them together even more firmly.
ja: ghiacciaの上で、Danteは髪が絡み合うほどに固く凍りついた二人の人物に問いかけ、その涙が凍りついて彼らをさらに強固に結びつけていく様を見つめる。

## Scene 52-69: The Deaf Sinner’s Revelation
en: On the *ghiaccia*, *Camiscion de' Pazzi* asks *Dante* why he stares at *cotesti due*, identifying them as the sons of *Alberto* from *Bisenzo* and claiming they are more deserving of the ice than any of *l'ombre*, including *l'ombra*, *quelli a cui fu rotto il petto* by *Artù*, and the nearby *Sassol Mascheroni*. He identifies himself as *Camiscion de' Pazzi* and states that he awaits *Carlin*.
ja: 氷原にて、Camiscion de' PazziはDanteに、なぜcotesti dueを凝視しているのかと問い、彼らがBisenzoのAlbertoの息子たちであり、l'ombraやArtùに胸を砕かれたquelli a cui fu rotto il petto、そして傍らのSassol Mascheroniを含むいかなるl'ombreよりも、彼らこそがこの氷にふさわしいと説いた。そして自らをCamiscion de' Pazziと名乗り、Carlinを待っているのだと告げた。

## Scene 70-78: Walking through the Frozen Multitude
en: *Dante* feels a lasting repulsion at the faces on the *ghiaccia*. As he and *Virgilio* walk toward the center, he trembles from the eternal shrieking and accidentally steps on a face.
ja: Danteは、ghiacciaに浮かぶ顔々に拭い去れぬ嫌悪感を抱いていた。Virgilioと共に中心へと歩む道中、彼は永遠に続く絶叫に身を震わせ、不意にある顔を踏みつけてしまう。

## Scene 79-84: The Scolding Sinner
en: Weeping, *il peccatore* scolds *Dante* for bothering him on the *ghiaccia*. *Dante* tells *Virgilio* to wait a moment so that he may resolve a doubt concerning *il peccatore*.
ja: 涙を流しながら、il peccatoreは氷原で自分を煩わせたDanteを咎めた。Danteはil peccatoreに関する疑問を解消するため、Virgilioにしばし待つよう告げた。

## Scene 85-96: The Verbal Dispute
en: In *l'Antenora*, *Virgilio* pauses while *Dante* asks *Ugolino* who he is. *Ugolino* responds with hostility, rejecting *Dante*'s offer to immortalize his name for fame and telling him to leave.
ja: DanteがUgolinoに正体を問う間、Virgilioは足を止めていた。Ugolinoは敵意をあらわにして応じ、名声を求めて名を不朽にするというDanteの申し出を拒絶し、立ち去るよう言い放った。

## Scene 97-105: The Physical Altercation
en: In *l'Antenora*, *Dante* seizes *il peccatore* by the scalp, threatening to rip out his hair unless he reveals his identity. When *il peccatore* stubbornly refuses, *Dante* pulls out several locks of hair while *il peccatore* barks with his eyes cast down.
ja: l'Antenoraにて、Danteはil peccatoreの頭皮を掴み、正体を明かさなければ髪をむしり取ると脅した。il peccatoreが頑なに拒むと、Danteは数束の髪をむしり取り、il peccatoreは視線を落としたまま吠えた。

## Scene 106-114: The Interruption by Bocca
en: In *l'Antenora*, *L'altro* ridicules *Bocca*, asking what *diavol* is troubling him, thereby revealing the traitor's name to *Dante*. *Dante* tells *Bocca* he now has the news to record his shame, and *Bocca* tells *Dante* to go away but to mention *L'altro* if he ever returns to the *lago*.
ja: l'Antenoraにて、L'altroがBoccaを嘲笑い、どのようなdiavolが彼を悩ませているのかと問うことで、Danteにその裏切り者の名が明かされる。Danteはお前の恥辱を書き記すための知らせを得たのだと告げると、BoccaはDanteに立ち去れと言い放ちながらも、もし再びlagoに戻ってくることがあればL'altroの名を伝えてくれと告げた。

## Scene 115-123: The Catalog of Traitors
en: In *l'Antenora*, *Bocca* tells *Dante* to record that *il peccatore* weeps for the silver of *Franceschi* and to mention the fate of *Beccheria*. He further speculates that *Gianni de' Soldanier* is frozen deeper in the *lago* with *Ganellone* and *Tebaldello*.
ja: l'Antenoraにおいて、BoccaはDanteに、il peccatoreがFranceschiの銀を惜しんで泣いていることを記録し、Beccheriaの運命についても言及するようにと告げる。さらに、Gianni de' SoldanierはGanelloneやTebaldelloと共に、lagoのさらに深い場所で凍りついているのではないかと彼は推測した。

## Scene 124-132: The Biting Sinners
en: Having departed from *Bocca*, *Dante* and *Virgilio* see *due ghiacciati* in *l'Antenora*, where *'l sovran* is biting the other's skull. *Dante* likens the scene to the way *Tidëo* once gnawed the temples of *Menalippo*.
ja: Boccaを後にしたDanteとVirgilioは、l'Antenoraで'l sovranがもう一方の頭蓋骨を噛んでいるdue ghiacciatiを目にする。Danteはその光景を、かつてTidëoがMenalippoの側頭部をかじった様子に例えた。

## Scene 133-139: Dante’s Inquiry into Hatred
en: In *l'Antenora* of the *lago*, *Dante* asks *l'ombra che morde* why he shows such bestial hatred toward *l'ombra morsa*. He offers to reveal the identities and sins of *le due ombre* to the world above, provided *l'ombra che morde* does not fall silent.
ja: l'Antenoraのlagoにて、Danteはl'ombra che mordeに、なぜl'ombra morsaへこれほどまで獣のような憎しみを向けるのかと問いかける。そして、l'ombra che mordeが沈黙しないのであれば、le due ombreの正体と罪を地上の世界に伝えようと申し出た。
